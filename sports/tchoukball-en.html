Tchoukball

No interfering with offense or defense — the fairest sport there is

Introduction

What Is Tchoukball?

A team sport where you throw the ball at a frame and score if the opponent fails to catch the rebound

  • 1
    Non-contact sport — All interference with opponents is prohibited by the rules. Intercepting, blocking, and tackling are all fouls!
  • 2
    The pinnacle of fair play — You cannot interfere with an opponent who is attacking. You can only try to catch the rebounded ball
  • 3
    Both frames are open — Either team can attack either frame, creating diverse tactical possibilities
UNESCO designated it the "most educational sport" — a model of sportsmanship that respects the opponent
History · Development

History of Tchoukball

Born from a scientist's quest to create an injury-free sport

  • 1
    Born in Switzerland, 1970 — Sports medicine specialist Dr. Hermann Brandt designed it after researching the question "Can we eliminate injuries from sports?"
  • 2
    Origin of the name — "Tshouk" means "shoot" in Swiss dialect, derived from the action of throwing at the frame
  • 3
    FITB established — The Federation Internationale de Tchoukball was founded, developing it into an international sport
  • 4
    Great popularity in Asia — Rapidly spread as a school sport in Asian countries including Taiwan, Singapore, and Korea
  • 5
    Educational value recognized — Rated as an ideal school sport for its injury-minimizing design and fair play philosophy
Rules · Equipment

Court and Equipment

Learn the structure of the tchoukball court and key equipment

  • 1
    Court — 26~29m x 15~17m, can be scaled down for school use
  • 2
    Frame — An angled trampoline-like net (55° angle), one on each end
  • 3
    Forbidden zone — A semicircular zone in front of the frame (3m radius), players cannot enter
  • 4
    Ball — A handball-sized ball is used
Rules

Basic Rules

Learn the core rules of tchoukball

⚡ Game Operations

- Official 7-a-side (5-a-side possible for school)
- Must shoot within 3 passes
- Throw the ball at the frame; the opposing team must catch it
- Failed catch = point for the throwing team!

🚫 Foul Situations

- Entering the forbidden zone (semicircle) is a foul
- A shot that misses the frame = point for the opposing team
- Cannot take more than 3 steps while holding the ball
- Interfering with opponents during their attack is an immediate foul

Rules · Unique

Unique Rules

Special rules found only in tchoukball and no other sport

  • ★
    Absolutely no interfering with opponent's attack — Intercepting, blocking, and pass disruption are all fouls. You must watch until the opponent shoots!
  • 2
    Both frames open for attack — Either team can attack either frame. However, the same frame can only be attacked 3 consecutive times
  • 3
    3-step rule — Taking more than 3 steps while holding the ball is a foul. Quick passing is essential!
  • 4
    Self-rebound penalty — If the rebounded ball returns to the shooter, the opposing team scores
Core philosophy — A sport focused not on beating the opponent, but on improving your own team's skills
Skills 1

Shooting

  • 1
    Overhand shot — The most basic. Throw hard at the frame from above the head
  • 2
    Sidearm shot — Throwing from the side. Makes it harder for the defense to predict
  • 3
    Jump shot — Throw at a high angle while jumping to change the rebound angle
  • 4
    Target the frame edges — Hitting the edges creates unpredictable rebounds
Skills 2

Catching

  • 1
    Predict the rebound — Read the ball's trajectory based on the frame angle and shot position
  • 2
    Two-hand catch — The most stable method. Wrap both hands around the ball
  • 3
    Diving catch — Dive toward low balls to make the catch
  • 4
    Body catch — Secure the ball against your chest for a reliable hold
  • 5
    Position distribution — Divide zones among teammates to cover different rebound directions
Skills 3

Passing

Key techniques for creating shooting opportunities within 3 passes

  • 1
    Chest pass — Push out from chest height with both hands. The basic pass — accurate and fast
  • 2
    Bounce pass — Bounce once off the floor. Effective at lowering the defense's attention
  • 3
    Overhead pass — Throw high over the head to reach distant teammates
  • 4
    Quick pass chains — Consecutive passes to disrupt the opponent's defensive formation
  • 5
    3-pass strategy — Since rules allow only 3 passes, efficient connections are the key to victory
Tactics · Strategy

Tactics

Tactical approaches to offense and defense

⚔️ Offensive Tactics

- Quick passing — Swift attacks utilizing the 3-pass rule
- Frame selection — Target the frame with weaker defense
- Create angles — Use jump/sidearm shots for unpredictable rebounds
- Fake shots — Pretend to throw to disrupt defensive timing

🛡️ Defensive Tactics

- Predict rebounds — Anticipate ball trajectory from shot angle and frame position
- Positioning — Distribute teammates evenly in a fan shape in front of the frame
- Cover open spaces — Move quickly based on the shooter's position
- Communication — Call out zones to teammates to prevent overlap

Safety

Safety Guidelines

Precautions for safe and enjoyable tchoukball

⚠️ Cautions

- Frame collision warning — Maintain distance to avoid running into the frame after shooting
- Sliding into forbidden zone — Be careful not to slide into the forbidden zone while moving quickly
- Finger injuries — Use both hands to catch strong rebounds to prevent fingers from bending back

🏃 Warm-up

- Shoulder stretching — Shooting heavily uses the shoulders, so warm them up well
- Wrist rotations — Both catching and shooting stress the wrists. Warm-up is essential
- Lower body stretching — Prepare for quick direction changes and diving
- Light pass practice — Exchange gentle passes with teammates before the game
