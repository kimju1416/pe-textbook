Futsal

Brilliant technique on a small court — the charm of indoor soccer

Introduction

What is Futsal?

A 5-a-side indoor soccer variant, a globally popular FIFA-sanctioned sport

  • 1
    5-a-Side Indoor Soccer — Five players including a goalkeeper form a team to play on a small indoor court. An official FIFA-sanctioned sport.
  • 2
    Smaller, Heavier Ball — Uses a size 4 ball, smaller than a regular size 5 soccer ball, designed with reduced bounce requiring precise foot technique.
  • 3
    Skill and Quick Decision-Making — With rapid offensive-defensive transitions in tight spaces, individual skill and split-second judgment matter far more than in outdoor soccer.
  • 4
    Worldwide Popularity — Beloved worldwide, especially in South America (Brazil, Argentina). Active leagues in Europe and Asia too.
Players 5 vs 5 Ball Size 4 low-bounce Court Gymnasium-sized FIFA-sanctioned sport
History

History of Futsal

From South American street soccer to a sport that captivated the world

  • 1
    1930, Uruguay — PE teacher Juan Carlos Ceriani created an indoor version of soccer. 'Futbol de Salon (living-room soccer)' was the origin of futsal.
  • 2
    South American Street Soccer Culture — Futsal exploded in popularity on Brazil's narrow streets and indoor courts, forming a unique skill-focused soccer culture.
  • 3
    Cradle of Soccer Stars — World-class soccer stars like Messi, Ronaldinho, Neymar, and Ronaldo all honed their skills through futsal as children.
  • 4
    1989 FIFA Futsal World Cup — The first FIFA Futsal World Cup was held in the Netherlands, establishing futsal as an international sport. Held every 4 years since.
  • 5
    Futsal in Korea — The K-Futsal League operates domestically, and futsal is spreading rapidly through amateur clubs and school sports clubs.
Court · Facilities

Futsal Court

Games are played on a rectangular court the size of a school gymnasium

  • 📐
    Court Size — 25–42m × 16–25m. Perfectly fits a standard school gymnasium.
  • 🥅
    Goal — 3m wide × 2m tall. Much smaller than soccer goals (7.32m × 2.44m).
  • ⭕
    Penalty Area — A 6m semicircular zone from the center of the goal. Only the GK can use hands here.
  • 🔴
    2nd Penalty Mark — At 10m from goal. Direct free kicks without a wall are taken here when accumulated fouls reach 6+.
Rules · Match

Match Rules

Learn the unique rules of futsal

⏱️ Match Duration

Two halves of 20 minutes each, using stopped clock (playing time) not running time. The clock stops when the ball goes out. School PE: 10 min per half.

⚽ Kickoff

Game starts and restarts from the center circle. The ball can be kicked in any direction; the kicker cannot touch it again before another player does.

⏰ 4-Second Rule

Kick-ins (soccer's throw-in), corner kicks, and goal clearances must be taken within 4 seconds. Exceeding the time = possession to the opponent!

🔙 One Back Pass Limit

Only one back pass to the GK is allowed. After the GK plays the ball, it cannot be returned until the opponent touches it.

⚡ Accumulated Fouls

Fouls accumulate per half. At 6 or more, the opponent gets a direct free kick from the 2nd penalty mark (10m) without a wall!

🔄 Free Substitution

Players can be freely substituted from the substitution zone without stopping play. No limit on substitutions — players can rotate in and out constantly.

Core Skill 1

Kicking

Various kicking techniques that shine on a small court

  • 1
    Toe Kick — A quick shot with the tip of the foot, futsal's signature shot. The minimal motion makes it hard for GKs to react.
  • 2
    Inside Kick — The most accurate kick using the inside of the foot. The basis for passes and close-range shots.
  • 3
    Instep Kick — A powerful shot with the top of the foot. Used for long-range goal attempts.
  • 4
    Heel Kick — A trick kick using the heel to pass backward or sideways. An advanced skill that disrupts opponent predictions.
  • 5
    Chipping — Lifting the ball gently off the ground. Used to loft over a downed GK or for wall passes.
Core Skill 2

Dribbling

The sole of the foot is key! Unique dribbling techniques of futsal

  • 1
    Sole Dribble — The core futsal skill! Step on and drag the ball with the sole, freely changing direction. The secret to not losing the ball in tight spaces.
  • 2
    Elastico — Push the ball out with the outside of the foot, then instantly pull it back in. Fools defenders side to side.
  • 3
    Roll (Sole Roll) — Roll the ball side to side or back and forth with the sole to steal the opponent's timing.
  • 4
    1v1 Situations — On a small court, 1v1 dribbling ability creates scoring chances! Combine fakes and dribbles to beat defenders.
Core Skill 3

Passing

Fast, accurate passing is the key to futsal victory

  • 1
    Wall Pass (One-Two) — Pass to a teammate and immediately run to receive the return pass. The core weapon for cutting through defenders.
  • 2
    Toe Pass — A quick touch pass using the toe. The minimal motion makes it hard for opponents to read.
  • 3
    Para — Stop the ball with the sole and immediately pass. Combines "stop + pass" into one motion.
  • 4
    Pivot Play (Back-to-Goal Receiving) — Receive the ball with your back to the opponent, then turn to pass or shoot. An advanced technique.
  • 5
    Space Pass — Pass into empty space where a teammate will run. Requires the vision to read teammate movements.
Tactics · Positions

Positions

4 field players + 1 goalkeeper — learn each role

🧤 Goalkeeper (Goleiro)

Not just shot-stopping — in futsal, the GK actively participates in attack! Accurate throws and kicks start fast counter-attacks.

🛡️ Fixo (Defender)

The defensive anchor at the back. During attacks, orchestrates play from behind with passes, providing stability.

⚡ Ala (Left/Right Wing)

Operates on the flanks, handling dribbling, passing, and shooting. A versatile position key to transitions.

🎯 Pivo (Post Player)

The central target at the front, with back to goal. Receives and connects play, or turns to shoot directly. The offensive focal point.

Formations: The basic 3-1 (1 fixo, 2 alas, 1 pivo) and 4-0 (no pivo, 4 players rotating freely) are the most common. Position rotation is the key to 4-0!
Tactics · Strategy

Futsal Tactics

Team tactics that create victories on a small court

🔄 Rotation

Four field players continuously swap positions. Confuses defensive marking and creates open spaces.

⚡ Power Play

Replace the GK with a 5th field player for an all-out attack. A desperate measure when trailing near the end!

🔥 Press (High Pressure)

Aggressively press in the opponent's half to win the ball back. Physically demanding but compensated by free substitutions.

💨 Counter-Attack

Quickly push forward after winning the ball. Score decisively with few players at speed!

🎯 Set Pieces

Planned movements during corner kicks and free kicks. Pre-arranged plays (signal plays) create scoring chances.

🧱 Defensive Organization

Switch between 1v1 man-marking and zone defense as needed. On a small court, one broken mark can mean danger!

Safety Guidelines

Playing Safely

Essential safety tips to enjoy futsal injury-free

🦶 Toe Injury Risk

Toe kicks put major impact on toes. Proper technique and futsal shoes are essential!

🦵 Knee Injury Risk

Frequent sharp direction changes and stops stress knee ligaments. Thorough warm-up and stretching are a must.

💥 Collision Risk

10 players on a small court = high collision risk. Always watch your surroundings; avoid reckless slides!

🏟️ Slippery Floor Warning

Indoor court floors can be slippery from sweat or moisture. Check floor conditions before the game and manage with towels.

👟 Futsal Shoes Required

Regular sneakers or cleats (studs) are strictly prohibited! Always wear futsal-specific indoor shoes (rubber sole, non-marking).

🤸 Warm-Up

Stretch ankles, knees, and thighs thoroughly! Lower body joint warm-ups are especially important given the frequent direction changes.

Etiquette · Sportsmanship

Futsal Etiquette

Good manners for an enjoyable game

  • 1
    Respect Your Opponents — No rough play, profanity, or arguing. Respect the referee's decisions and show courtesy to opponents.
  • 2
    Care for Injured Players — If an opponent is hurt, kick the ball out to stop play. It's good sportsmanship to return the ball to them at the next throw-in.
  • 3
    Fair Competition — Deliberate time-wasting and foul play go against the spirit of sport. Compete fairly with skill.
  • 4
    Post-Game Greetings — Win or lose, shake hands with the opposing team and referee, and leave the court clean.

  • Q2
    In futsal, kick-ins must be taken within 4 seconds. — True. Kick-ins, corner kicks, and goal clearances all must be taken within 4 seconds.
  • Q3
    Futsal uses a regular size 5 soccer ball. — False. Futsal uses a size 4 low-bounce ball.
  • Q4
    When a team's accumulated fouls reach 6+, the opponent gets a direct free kick without a wall. — True. At 6+ accumulated fouls per half, a direct free kick is taken from the 2nd penalty mark (10m) without a wall.
  • Q5
    Futsal goals are the same size as soccer goals. — False. Futsal goals are 3m x 2m, much smaller than soccer goals (7.32m x 2.44m).
