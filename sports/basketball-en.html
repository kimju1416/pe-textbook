Introduction

What is Basketball?

A 5-on-5 territorial competition sport — score points by putting the ball into the opponent's basket

  • 1
    Objective — Score more points by shooting the ball into the opponent's basket, mounted 3.05m above the floor
  • 2
    Players — 5 players per team on the court + 7 substitutes (12-player roster)
  • 3
    How to play — The ball can only be moved by dribbling, passing, or shooting (carrying the ball while running is a violation!)
1891 Invented by James Naismith Developed as a winter indoor sport by a YMCA PE teacher 450 million players worldwide
History

Fascinating History

From peach baskets to a global sport

  • 1891
    Basketball is born — James Naismith, a PE teacher at Springfield YMCA in the US, invented it as a winter indoor activity. The original game used peach baskets nailed to the gym wall and a soccer ball!
  • 1936
    Olympic debut — Men's basketball was first included as an official sport at the Berlin Olympics. Naismith himself attended the event.
  • 1946
    NBA founded — The NBA (originally BAA) was established in the US. It has grown into the world's premier basketball league.
  • 1907
    Introduced to Korea — Basketball was first brought to Korea through the Seoul YMCA.
  • 1997
    KBL launched — The Korean Basketball League (KBL) was founded. Currently 10 teams compete in the league.

In the early days, when a ball went into the basket, someone had to climb a ladder to get it out! Later, the bottom of the basket was cut open so the ball could fall through.

Rules · Game Play

Game Play Rules

Here's how a basketball game flows

⏱️ 4 Quarters

A regulation game consists of 4 quarters of 10 minutes each (40 minutes total). In school PE classes, quarters may be shortened to 6–8 minutes. 2-minute break between Q1 and Q2; 15-minute halftime.

🏁 Jump Ball Start

The game begins with a jump ball at center court! The referee tosses the ball up and one player from each team leaps to tap it.

⏳ 24-Second Shot Clock

Once a team gains possession, they must attempt a shot within 24 seconds. If time expires, they lose possession! Quick decision-making is essential.

🔄 Transition

When possession changes through a made basket, turnover, or rebound, the roles of offense and defense switch immediately.

🖐️ Timeouts

Coaches can stop the game to give tactical instructions. 2 timeouts available in the first half, 3 in the second half.

⚡ Overtime

If the score is tied after 4 quarters, a 5-minute overtime period is played! Overtime continues until a winner is determined.

Rules · Scoring

Scoring Methods

Points vary depending on where and how you score

  • 2
    2-Point Field Goal — A successful shot from inside the 3-point line. Includes layups, dunks, and mid-range shots.
  • 3
    3-Point Shot — A successful shot from beyond the 3-point line (6.75m). Worth 3 points, it's the key to comebacks!
  • 1
    Free Throw — Awarded when fouled during a shooting motion. Worth 1 point each, taken without interference.

PE class tip: Narrowing the 3-point line or awarding bonus points for layups can boost participation!

Tactics · Positions

5 Positions

Each player has a role to use the court efficiently

  • PG
    Point Guard — The team's floor general! Orchestrates the offense and distributes passes. Requires excellent court vision and dribbling skills.
  • SG
    Shooting Guard — A sharpshooter who scores with accurate outside shots. The 3-pointer and mid-range shot are primary weapons.
  • SF
    Small Forward — A versatile player who contributes on both offense and defense. Handles drives, shooting, and defense.
  • PF
    Power Forward — Uses physical strength near the basket for rebounds and close-range scoring.
  • C
    Center — Typically the tallest player on the team. The last line of defense near the basket, essential for rebounds and blocks.
Rules · Violations

Key Violations

Know the rules and you'll understand the game! A complete guide to common violations

🚶 Traveling

A player can only take up to 2 steps while holding the ball. More than that is a traveling violation. The most common mistake for beginners! Use the pivot (rotate with one foot fixed).

✋ Double Dribble

Stopping your dribble and then dribbling again is a violation. This also includes picking up the ball with both hands and then bouncing it again.

💥 Personal Foul

Physical contact like pushing, grabbing, or hitting the opponent. Foul during a shot attempt = free throws awarded. A player is ejected after 5 fouls!

⏱️ 3-Second Rule

Staying in the restricted area (paint zone) for more than 3 seconds while on offense is a violation. Move in and out quickly.

🖐️ 5-Second Rule

Failing to throw in (inbound) or take a free throw within 5 seconds results in loss of possession.

⏳ 8-Second Rule

Failing to advance the ball from the backcourt to the frontcourt within 8 seconds is a violation.

🔥 24-Second Rule

A team must attempt a shot within 24 seconds of gaining possession. This rule prevents stalling!

Core Skill 1

Dribbling

The fundamental skill for safely moving the ball up the court

  • 1
    Wrist Snap — Push the ball with your fingertips, not your palm. If the ball sticks to your palm, you lose control!
  • 2
    Low Stance — Bend your knees, lower your body, and bounce the ball below your waist. The lower you are, the harder it is to steal.
  • 3
    Eyes Up — Don't look at the ball — scan the court and find your teammates. It's difficult at first, but improves with practice!
  • 4
    Advanced Moves — Crossover (side-to-side), behind-the-back, and between-the-legs dribbles help you shake defenders.
Core Skill 2

Passing

Quickly and accurately connecting the ball to teammates

  • 1
    Chest Pass — Push the ball forward with both hands from chest level. The most basic pass — accurate and fast.
  • 2
    Bounce Pass — Bounce the ball at the 2/3 mark on the floor. Useful for passing underneath a defender's hands!
  • 3
    Overhead Pass — Throw with both hands from above the head. Used when passing over a tall defender.
  • 4
    Receiving — Extend your hands with fingers spread to meet the ball, absorb the impact, and pull it gently to your chest.
Core Skill 3

Shooting

Learn proper shooting form with the BEEF principle

  • B
    Balance — Feet shoulder-width apart, knees slightly bent for a stable stance. Place your shooting foot (right foot for right-handers) slightly forward.
  • E
    Eyes — Focus your eyes on the front of the rim! Looking at the target, not the ball, improves accuracy.
  • E
    Elbow — Keep your shooting arm's elbow at 90 degrees and aligned with your body. If the elbow flares out, the ball drifts sideways.
  • F
    Follow-through — Snap your wrist all the way through after releasing the ball! Maintain a "goose neck" shape for better accuracy.

Layup: A running shot close to the basket. Footwork: "right foot → left foot → jump" (for right-handers). Aim for the corner of the square on the backboard!

Jump shot: Jump from a standing position and release at the peak. The most commonly used shot because it avoids defenders.

Tactics

Basic Tactics

Individual skills + team tactics = the key to victory

⚡ Fast Break

Transition quickly to offense right after gaining possession! Score easy baskets before the opposing defense can set up.

🛡️ Zone Defense

Each player guards a designated area. The 2-3 zone (2 players up front, 3 in back) is the most basic. Effective for protecting the paint.

👤 Man-to-Man Defense

Each player closely guards one opponent 1-on-1. Physically demanding but enables strong pressure.

🧱 Screen (Pick)

Standing like a wall in front of a defender to help a teammate. Creates space for the teammate to shoot or drive.

🔄 Pick and Roll

After setting a screen, immediately cut toward the basket. The most commonly used offensive play in the NBA!

🎯 Cut

An off-ball player suddenly changes direction and drives toward the basket. A weapon that exploits gaps in the defense.

Safety Guidelines

Staying Safe While Playing

Enjoying the game injury-free is the most important thing!

🦶 Ankle & Knee Protection

Frequent jumping increases the risk of ankle sprains and knee injuries. Always warm up your ankles before playing, and land with bent knees to absorb impact. Be careful not to step on other players' feet.

🖐️ Finger Injuries

Fingers can get jammed when catching passes or rebounds incorrectly. Spread your palms wide to receive the ball, not just your fingertips. Be careful not to catch the ball with bent fingers.

💥 Collision Prevention

Moving fast in a small court increases collision risk! Always be aware of your surroundings and avoid excessive physical play. Maintain proper defensive position only.

✅ Preparation Checklist

Tie shoelaces tightly, trim nails short, use eyeglass straps if needed, stay hydrated, and always warm up for at least 10 minutes before playing!

True/False Quiz

  • Q1
    Basketball was invented by James Naismith in 1891 as a summer outdoor sport. — False. It was invented as a winter indoor sport.
  • Q2
    In basketball, a successful shot from beyond the 3-point line is worth 3 points. — True. Shots made from beyond the 3-point line (6.75m) earn 3 points.
  • Q3
    Taking 3 or more steps while holding the ball is a double dribble violation. — False. Taking 3 or more steps is a traveling violation. Double dribble is when you stop dribbling and then dribble again.
  • Q4
    A player who commits 5 fouls is ejected from the game. — True. A player is fouled out after accumulating 5 personal fouls.
  • Q5
    A basketball game starts with a free throw at center court. — False. It starts with a jump ball at the center circle.
