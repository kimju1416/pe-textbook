T-Ball

The first step to baseball — swing hard at the ball on the tee!

Introduction

What is T-Ball?

A baseball-style sport where you hit a stationary ball placed on a batting tee

  • 1
    Stationary Ball on a Tee — No pitcher in T-Ball. A ball is placed on a rubber batting tee at home plate, and the batter hits it directly. Everyone can hit, so participation is extremely high.
  • 2
    Modified Baseball — Same field competition structure as baseball, but no pitcher, walks, or (pitch-based) strikeouts, so rules are much simpler. Ideal for learning batting and fielding fundamentals.
  • 3
    Beginners Welcome — No need to hit a moving pitch, so students new to baseball can enjoy it without pressure. The stationary ball means high batting success and fast-paced games.
  • 4
    A Staple of School PE — Widely used in Korean middle and high school PE as a representative field competition sport. Safe and accessible for boys and girls alike.
  • 5
    Strategy and Teamwork — Looks simple, but fielding positions, batting order strategy, and baserunning decisions determine victory. Experience baseball's strategic fun firsthand.

History

History of T-Ball

From children's baseball introduction to a worldwide sport

  • 1
    1950s USA — Created to solve the problem of children struggling to hit pitched balls. Placing the ball on a tee so anyone could bat was the beginning.
  • 2
    1970s–80s: Global Spread — Adopted as an official introductory program by US Little League, then spread to Australia, New Zealand, Japan, etc.
  • 3
    Adopted in Korean Schools — After 2000, T-Ball spread nationwide through school sports clubs. Easy to play on any school field without a baseball diamond.
  • 4
    Part of the Curriculum — Currently included in Korea's PE curriculum as a core field competition sport. Popular in national school sports tournaments.
  • 5
    Tool for Inclusive PE — Simple rules and safety make it actively used in inclusive PE where students with and without disabilities play together.

Rules · Game Play

Game Rules

Learn the core rules of T-Ball

🔢 5-Inning Game

Regulation T-Ball: 5 innings. Each inning ends when both teams complete batting and fielding. School PE may use 3 innings.

🏏 Batting Tee Used

A batting tee is placed on home plate with the ball on top. The batter hits a stationary ball. Tee height is adjusted to the batter's waist.

❌ 3 Fouls = Out

No walks in T-Ball. Instead, 3 foul balls = batter out. No strike/ball calls from a pitcher; swings and misses count as strikes.

👥 Everyone Bats

In school PE, every team member bats. Some rules switch sides after everyone bats, even without 3 outs.

🏃 Baserunning Rules

After hitting, run to 1st. Runners can only advance when the batter hits (no stealing). Overrunning 2nd or 3rd can lead to tag outs.

🔄 Out Rules

1. Fly out: fielder catches ball before it hits ground
2. Force out: fielder with ball steps on base first
3. Tag out: fielder with ball touches the runner

Field · Equipment

T-Ball Field and Equipment

A small diamond for big fun

  • 💎
    T-Ball Diamond — Same diamond shape as baseball but 15–18m between bases (vs 27.43m). Fits small school fields easily.
  • 🏏
    Batting Tee — Height-adjustable rubber stand. Set to the batter's waist height. Place the ball on top and you're ready.
  • ⚾
    T-Ball Ball — A soft rubber safety ball unlike a hard baseball. Won't cause serious injury if hit, ideal for school PE.
  • 🧤
    Glove and Bat — Standard baseball gloves and lightweight aluminum or plastic bats. Choose bat weight appropriate for body size.
Core Skill 1

Batting

The core of T-Ball — hitting a stationary ball accurately

  • 1
    Adjust Tee Height — Set the tee to your waist-to-navel height. Too high or low makes the swing unnatural and hard to control direction.
  • 2
    Grip — Hold the bat naturally with both hands. Right-handers: left hand below, right hand above. Don't grip too tightly.
  • 3
    Stance — Stand beside home plate, feet shoulder-width, knees slightly bent. Eyes on the ball, weight evenly distributed.
  • 4
    Level Swing — Transfer power: hips → arms → bat. Hit the center of the ball with a level swing for a strong line drive. Swinging down = grounder; scooping up = pop fly.
  • 5
    Follow-Through — Complete the swing naturally. Wrap the bat behind your shoulder for full power transfer.
Core Skill 2

Fielding and Throwing

Catch, throw, and make outs

  • 1
    Basic Fielding Stance — Feet shoulder-width, knees bent, low position. Glove out front toward the ball. Cover with the other hand on catch.
  • 2
    Glove Technique — Above waist: glove up. Below waist: glove down. Catch in front of your body for safety.
  • 3
    Accurate Throwing — After catching, throw to the target base accurately. Crow step (crossover) for momentum; throw overhand with power and precision.
  • 4
    Base Coverage — When the ball is hit, each fielder moves to cover their assigned base. The fielder receiving the throw stands on the base in ready position.
  • 5
    Fly Ball Catching — Move quickly to the landing spot and catch overhead with both hands. Call out "I got it!" to prevent collisions with teammates.
Core Skill 3

Base Running

Sprinting around the bases after hitting

  • 1
    Run Immediately After Hitting — Drop the bat (don't throw it!) and sprint to 1st base immediately. Don't watch where the ball went — just run.
  • 2
    Touch Each Base — You must step on each base while running. Missing a base can result in an out on appeal.
  • 3
    1st Base Overrun — 1st is the only base where you can run through without being tagged. Don't slow down — run through and veer into foul territory.
  • 4
    Advancing to 2nd/3rd — Judge whether to advance based on where the ball went. Follow the coach's (or teammate's) signals: "Stop!" or "Go!" Reckless advancing causes outs.
  • 5
    Avoiding Tags — When a fielder tries to tag you between bases, use quick stops and direction changes to evade. But going more than ~91cm (3 ft) off the baseline = automatic out.
T-Ball baserunning special rule: No stealing allowed. Runners can only leave for the next base when the batter hits. Leading off is also prohibited.
Safety Guidelines

Playing Safely

Bats and balls require strict safety rules

🏏 Bat Swing Distance

Always check surroundings before swinging. On-deck batters and spectators stay 3m+ away. Set the bat down carefully — don't throw it.

⛑️ Batter Helmet Required

All batters and baserunners must wear helmets. Prevents injuries from errant throws or batted balls.

⚾ Watch for Batted Balls

Strong hits can strike fielders. Always watch the ball's direction. The fielder at the pitcher position should especially stay ready.

🚫 No Sliding

In school PE, sliding is prohibited. Diving into bases risks knee and wrist injuries. Stand and step on the base safely.

🤸 Warm-Up Required

Stretch shoulders, elbows, wrists, back, and knees. Shoulder stretching and hip rotation prevent batting/throwing injuries.

🌞 Check Environment

Before play, check for rocks, glass, or debris on the field. In summer, stay hydrated and protect against UV.

Quiz

True/False Quiz

Let's check what you've learned!

  • Q1
    T-Ball is a sport where you hit a pitcher's throw with a bat. — False. T-Ball has no pitcher. You hit a stationary ball placed on a batting tee.
  • Q2
    T-Ball games usually have 5 innings. — True. Regulation is 5 innings; school PE may use 3.
  • Q3
    In T-Ball, 3 foul balls result in an out. — True. Unlike baseball, 3 accumulated fouls = batter out.
  • Q4
    In T-Ball, runners can steal bases anytime. — False. Stealing is prohibited. Runners can only leave when the batter hits.
  • Q5
    1st base allows overrunning without being tagged out. — True. 1st is the only base where overrunning is allowed. However, showing intent to go to 2nd makes you eligible for a tag.
