Introduction

What is Softball?

A field competition sport — baseball's sister sport with a bigger ball and shorter distances for faster play

  • 1
    Baseball's Sister Sport — Similar rules to baseball but with a larger ball (30.5cm circumference) and shorter base distances (18.3m), making for faster, more dynamic play.
  • 2
    Underhand Pitching — The biggest difference from baseball! The pitcher throws underhand (windmill motion), swinging the arm from below to above.
  • 3
    7-Inning Game — Shorter than baseball's 9 innings, allowing faster-paced, more focused games.

History

History of Softball

From indoor baseball in Chicago to the Olympic stage

  • 1887
    Softball is born — Created in Chicago by reporter George Hancock. On Thanksgiving, players used a boxing glove as a ball and a broom as a bat in a gym — the origin of 'Indoor Baseball.'
  • 1926
    Name established — Previously called mushball, kittenball, diamond ball, etc., the game was officially unified under "Softball."
  • 1952
    ISF founded — The International Softball Federation was established to govern rules and tournaments worldwide.
  • 1996
    Olympic sport — Women's softball became official at the Atlanta Olympics! The US won the first gold.
  • 2020
    Tokyo Olympics revival — Dropped after 2008, softball returned at the 2020 Tokyo Olympics as a host-nation added sport. Japan won gold!

Korean women's softball has won multiple Asian Games medals and is a respected force in Asia. It's also a popular school PE sport.

Rules

Game Rules

How a softball game is played

🥎 7 Innings

Regulation: 7 innings. School PE: 3–5 innings. Each inning has a top (batting) and bottom (fielding) half.

🔄 3 Outs = Side Change

3 outs and teams switch. Strikeouts, fly outs, ground outs — various ways to record outs.

⚾ Underhand Only

The defining feature! Pitchers must throw underhand only. Overhand or sidearm = illegal pitch.

🏃 Stealing Restriction

Unlike baseball, runners cannot leave the base until the pitcher releases. Stealing timing is critical!

🔁 Pinch Hitter/Runner

Substitute batters (pinch hitters) and fast runners (pinch runners) can be used strategically.

⏱️ Tiebreaker

Tied after 7? Extra innings! A runner starts on 2nd base (tiebreaker rule) to encourage quick resolution.

Core Skill 1

Batting

The big ball seems easier, but timing matters more

  • 1
    Grip — Align knuckles and wrap the bat. Don't grip too tightly — it stiffens the swing.
  • 2
    Stance — Feet shoulder-width, face home plate, knees slightly bent, weight on back foot.
  • 3
    Slap Hitting — A unique softball technique! Run while hitting from the left side to aim for infield hits. Uses the short base distance strategically.
  • 4
    Bunt — Deaden the ball for a short roller. Sacrifice bunt advances runners; safety bunt aims for a hit.

The ball is big but the pitching distance is short, so reaction time is similar to baseball. Timing practice against the underhand arc is most important!

Core Skill 2

Pitching — Windmill

Softball's unique pitching with a 360-degree arm rotation

  • 1
    Windmill Motion — A full 360-degree arm rotation like a windmill. Shoulder flexibility and precise release point are key.
  • 2
    Riseball — The ball rises in front of the batter. Only possible with underhand delivery — softball's signature pitch! Heavy backspin makes it soar.
  • 3
    Dropball — Drops sharply in front of the batter. Topspin makes it plunge, inducing swings and misses.
  • 4
    Changeup — Thrown with the same motion but much slower. Steals the batter's timing — bigger speed difference = more effective.

Fastpitch speed: ~100–110 km/h! At 13.1m distance, the perceived speed equals ~160 km/h in baseball terms.

Core Skill 3

Fielding

Softball fielding demands fast decisions at short range

  • 1
    Catching — Extend glove toward the ball, wrap it securely on contact. The larger softball ball must be caught firmly.
  • 2
    Throwing — After catching, throw quickly to the target base. Accuracy matters more than speed at short distances! Step toward the target first.
  • 3
    Double Play — Two outs on one play! Shorter base distances make double plays more common than in baseball.
  • 4
    Catcher's Role — Must handle the low-trajectory underhand pitch. Coordinates with the pitcher via signals; prevents stealing too.

Short base distances make infielder reaction speed crucial. Always maintain a ready position!

Tactics

Softball Tactics

Short distances and fast play create strategic depth

🏃 Bunt and Run

Runner takes off with the pitch; batter bunts. Runner advances while defense handles the bunt. A fundamental softball tactic!

⚡ Slap Hitting

Left-handed batter runs while hitting for infield hits. Uses short base distance to beat the throw to 1st.

🏃‍♂️ Steal Timing

Can only steal after the pitcher releases. Reading the release point precisely is key — a good jump is 80% of success!

🛡️ Defensive Shift

Adjust fielding positions based on batter tendencies. Strengthen the pull side for pull hitters.

🔄 Double Play Setup

With runners on, induce ground balls to get two outs at once. Short bases favor double plays.

🎯 Pitching Changes

In fastpitch, the pitcher's impact is enormous. Strategically swap pitchers based on the opponent's left/right batter ratio.

Safety Guidelines

Staying Safe While Playing

Safety comes first for an enjoyable game

⛑️ Batting Helmet

Always wear a batting helmet when at bat! Keep it on while running bases. Errant pitches and foul balls are constant risks.

🥽 Catcher's Gear

Catchers must have mask, chest protector, and shin guards. High risk of foul tips and bat contact — never catch without full gear!

🦵 Sliding Safety

Wear sliding pads. Learn proper form (slide on hips, not feet first) to prevent injuries.

💪 Pitching Form & Shoulder Care

Windmill pitching stresses the shoulder joint. Improper form + overuse = injury risk! Gradual practice and stretching are essential.

↔️ Maintain Distance

A swinging bat is very dangerous. Ensure safety distance around the batter; on-deck batters practice only in designated areas.

✅ Preparation Checklist

Tie shoes tight, check glove condition, check bat grip, hydrate, warm up 10+ minutes!
