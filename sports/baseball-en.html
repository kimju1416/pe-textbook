Introduction

What is Baseball / T-Ball?

A field competition sport where batters hit a pitched ball and run the bases to score

  • 1
    Field Competition Sport — The batting team hits a ball pitched by the pitcher and advances around bases (1st→2nd→3rd→Home) to score, while the fielding team tries to prevent it. 9 players per team.
  • 2
    9-Inning Game — Teams alternate between batting and fielding; one cycle = 1 inning. Regulation games have 9 innings; the team with more runs wins.
  • 3
    Korea's National Pastime (KBO) — The Korean Baseball Organization (KBO) league, founded in 1982, is Korea's most popular professional sport, drawing millions of fans yearly.
  • 4
    T-Ball = Modified Baseball — T-Ball has no pitcher; batters hit a ball placed on a rubber tee (stand). Everyone can hit, making it ideal for school PE.
  • 5
    T-Ball Focus in School — Lower risk from pitching/catching, higher hitting success rate so all students enjoy participating. Keeps baseball's strategic fun while improving accessibility.

History

History of Baseball

From America to the world — a bat and ball sport

  • 1
    1839 Doubleday Myth & 1845 Cartwright — Legend credits Abner Doubleday with inventing baseball, but Alexander Cartwright is recognized for codifying modern rules in 1845 (diamond shape, 3-out rotation, foul lines, etc.).
  • 2
    1905: Introduced to Korea — American missionary Philip Gillett brought baseball to Korea. It took root as a school sport.
  • 3
    KBO League (1982~) — Korea's pro baseball started with 6 teams, now expanded to 10. Millions attend annually — Korea's top professional sport.
  • 4
    WBC and Olympics — The World Baseball Classic (baseball's World Cup) started in 2006. Baseball returned as an Olympic sport at the 2020 Tokyo Games.
  • 5
    T-Ball's Spread — Through school sports clubs, T-Ball has spread nationwide, letting students who couldn't access baseball experience field competition.
Field

Baseball Field

A diamond-shaped infield and wide outfield

  • 💎
    Diamond — The infield is a square (27.43m sides) arranged as a diamond. T-Ball reduces base distance to 18m.
  • ⚾
    Pitcher's Plate — On the mound, 18.44m from home. Pitcher must have a foot on the plate when pitching. T-Ball has no pitcher's plate.
  • 📐
    Foul Lines — Lines extending from home through 1st and 3rd bases. Balls hit inside are fair (in play); outside are foul.
  • 🏟️
    Dugout — Team bench areas on the 1st base and 3rd base sides.
Rules · Game Play

Game Rules

Innings, strikes, balls — core rules of baseball and T-Ball

🔢 Innings

Baseball: 9 innings (1 inning = each team bats and fields once). T-Ball: 5–7 innings; school PE often uses 3 innings.

🔄 3 Outs = Side Change

When the fielding team records 3 outs, teams switch. The batting team continues batting until they get 3 outs.

🎯 3 Strikes = Strikeout

If a batter fails to hit a pitch in the strike zone or swings and misses, it's a strike. 3 strikes = strikeout (out).

⚾ 4 Balls = Walk

If the pitcher throws 4 balls outside the zone, the batter walks to 1st base. Also called a base on balls (BB).

📏 Foul Ball

A hit ball outside the foul line is foul. Fouls count as strikes up to 2 strikes only; additional fouls don't count (except bunts).

🏏 T-Ball Special Rules

No pitcher — batter hits a ball placed on a rubber tee. No walks; every batter gets to hit. Strikes only from swings and misses.

Rules · Outs & Advancing

Outs and Base Running

The core of offense and defense — how to get outs and stay alive

🫳 Strikeout

3 strikes and the batter is out. If the catcher drops the 3rd strike, the batter can run to 1st ('uncaught third strike').

🧤 Fly Out

If a fielder catches a hit ball before it touches the ground, the batter is immediately out.

✋ Tag Out

A fielder with the ball tags a runner not on a base = out. Common during steal attempts or baserunning errors.

👟 Force Out

When a runner must advance, a fielder with the ball steps on the base first = out.

⚡ Double Play

Two outs on one play. E.g., shortstop fields a grounder, throws to 2nd, relay to 1st for two outs.

🏃 Steals & Sacrifice Plays

Steal: Runner sprints to next base during the pitch. Sacrifice bunt: Batter bunts to advance a runner at the cost of an out. Sacrifice fly: Outfield fly that scores a runner from 3rd.

Positions

9 Positions

Understanding each position makes baseball more fun

  • 1
    Pitcher (P) — Throws to the batter from the mound. Controls the game's pace with various pitches (fastball, breaking balls).
  • 2
    Catcher (C) — Behind home plate, receiving pitches. Signals pitch selection to the pitcher; the defensive leader.
  • 3
    First Baseman (1B) — Guards 1st base, receives throws from infielders to put out batters.
  • 4
    Second Baseman (2B) — Covers between 1st and 2nd. Quick feet and agility; center of double plays.
  • 5
    Shortstop (SS) — Between 2nd and 3rd — the infield's star. Requires wide range, strong arm, quick decisions. The toughest position.
  • 6
    Third Baseman (3B) — Guards 3rd base, handles hard-hit balls ('hot corner'). Quick reactions and strong throws needed.
  • 7
    Left Field (LF) · Center Field (CF) · Right Field (RF) — Cover the outfield in thirds. CF covers the most ground and leads outfield defense; RF needs a strong arm for throws to 3rd.
In T-Ball? No pitcher, so the pitcher position is replaced by a fielder, or team size is adjusted.
Core Skill 1

Batting

The flower of baseball — accurately connecting bat to ball

  • 1
    Grip — Hold the bat with both hands naturally, as if knocking on a door. Don't grip too tightly — it stiffens the swing.
  • 2
    Stance — Feet shoulder-width apart, knees slightly bent, comfortable posture. Eyes on the pitcher, weight evenly distributed.
  • 3
    Swing — Power transfers: hips → arms → bat. Bring the bat to the ball on the shortest path. Keep your eyes on the ball throughout.
  • 4
    Follow-Through — Complete the swing naturally after contact. Stopping abruptly reduces power transfer and risks injury.
  • 5
    Bunt — Hold the bat out to softly direct the ball. Sacrifice bunt advances runners; safety bunt aims for an infield hit.
  • 6
    T-Ball Batting Tips — Adjust tee to waist height, focus on the ball, and swing level. No pitcher means you can focus purely on hitting mechanics.
Core Skill 2

Pitching

Game-controlling skills of the pitcher

  • 1
    Windup — Used with no runners. A full rotational motion to generate maximum velocity.
  • 2
    Set Position — Used with runners on. More compact to help prevent steals.
  • 3
    Fastball — The fastest, most fundamental pitch. Pros throw 140–160 km/h. The foundation for all other pitches.
  • 4
    Breaking Balls — Curveball & Changeup — Curveball breaks down sharply; changeup comes slower from the same motion. Both steal the batter's timing.
  • 5
    Strike Zone — The area between the batter's chest-waist midpoint and below the knees, over home plate. Pitches through this zone are called strikes.
In T-Ball? No pitcher, so focus on fielding position, quick throws, and team communication instead.
Core Skill 3

Fielding

Catching, throwing, and making outs

  • 1
    Glove Catch — Open glove toward the ball; cover with other hand when it enters. Above waist = glove up; below = glove down.
  • 2
    Fly Catch — Judge trajectory, move to the landing spot, catch overhead with both hands. Call "I got it!" to prevent collisions.
  • 3
    Ground Ball — Low stance, glove on the ground, field the ball in front of your body. Processing it centered is safest.
  • 4
    Throwing — After catching, throw accurately to the target base. Use a crow hop for momentum; throw overhand with power and accuracy.
  • 5
    Tag — Touch the runner with the gloved ball for a tag out. Anticipate the runner's sliding direction and present the glove low.
Tactics

Baseball / T-Ball Tactics

9 players moving as one team with strategic thinking

📋 Batting Order

1st–2nd batters have good on-base skills; 3rd–5th are power hitters (cleanup). Batting order is the starting point of team strategy.

🤏 Bunt Tactics

Sacrifice bunt advances runners at the cost of an out; safety bunt aims for an infield hit. Used by pitchers or fast runners.

🏃‍♂️ Stolen Base

A fast runner sprints to the next base during the pitch. Success greatly increases scoring chances, but failure = out.

🔗 Relay (Cutoff) Play

When outfielders can't throw to the base directly, relay: outfielder → infield cutoff → base. Precise positioning is key.

↔️ Defensive Shift

Shift fielding positions based on a batter's hitting tendencies. E.g., stack defenders to the pull side for a pull-hitter.

⚾ T-Ball Team Tactics

Batters aim for gaps; fielders focus on position coverage and backup. Without a pitcher, fielding teamwork is even more crucial.

Safety Guidelines

Playing Safely

Bats and balls require strict safety awareness

🏏 Bat Safety

Always check surroundings before swinging. On-deck batters stay 3m+ away. Never throw the bat after hitting.

⚾ Ball Safety

Watch for hit-by-pitch and unpredictable foul balls. Always track the ball's direction during play.

🦵 Sliding Caution

Sliding into bases risks knee and wrist injuries. Learn proper form (hips→thigh landing); avoid reckless slides.

💥 Collision Prevention

Prevent runner-fielder and outfielder-outfielder collisions by calling out "Mine!" Home plate collisions are now banned by rule.

⛑️ Helmets Required

Batters and all runners must wear helmets. Catchers need mask, chest protector, and leg guards.

🤸 Warm-Up

Stretch shoulders, elbows, wrists, back, and knees. Shoulder stretching is especially critical before throwing.

Quiz

True/False Quiz

Let's check what you've learned!

  • Q1
    A batter with 3 strikes is called a strikeout. — True. 3 strikes = strikeout. 4 balls = walk to 1st base.
  • Q2
    A baseball game has 7 innings. — False. Regulation baseball has 9 innings; T-Ball typically has 5–7.
  • Q3
    Foul balls never count as strikes. — False. Fouls count as strikes up to 2. After 2 strikes, additional fouls don't add strikes (except bunts).
  • Q4
    T-Ball uses a rubber tee instead of a pitcher. — True. No pitcher; every batter can hit, making it ideal for school PE.
  • Q5
    On a home run, all runners on base score. — True. When the ball clears the fence, the batter and all baserunners come home to score.
