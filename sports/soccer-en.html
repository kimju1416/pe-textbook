Soccer

The world's most beloved sport — drama that starts at the tip of your foot

Introduction

What is Soccer?

An 11-a-side territorial competition sport — score by getting the ball into the opponent's goal using any body part except the hands

  • 1
    11 players per team — 1 goalkeeper + 10 field players. Only the goalkeeper can use hands, and only inside their penalty box
  • 2
    Two halves of 45 minutes — 90 minutes total (school PE classes can adapt to 20 min x 2, etc.). 15-minute halftime break
  • 3
    Offside rule — A unique tactical rule in soccer that prevents attackers from waiting too close to the goal ("cherry-picking")
FIFA member nations: 211 World Cup viewers: 5 billion+ World's #1 most popular sport For everyone, all ages
History

Fascinating History

From ancient ball-kicking games to the world's biggest sport

  • 🏺
    Ancient Chinese Cuju — Ball-kicking for military training dating back 2,000 years to China's Han Dynasty. FIFA officially recognizes it as the origin of soccer
  • 🏴
    1863: England's FA founded — 12 clubs in London gathered to create unified rules. The birth of modern soccer! Those who wanted to use their hands split off to form rugby
  • 🏆
    1930: First FIFA World Cup — Held in Uruguay with 13 nations participating. Host nation Uruguay won! Now 48 countries participate (expanded from 2026)
  • 🇰🇷
    2002: Korea-Japan World Cup — The greatest moment in Korean soccer history! The semifinal miracle. The "Dae~han-min-guk!" chants amazed the whole world
  • ⚽
    K League (1983~) — Asia's first professional soccer league. Currently operates K League 1 (12 teams) + K League 2 (13 teams)
  • 👩
    Growth of women's soccer — The first FIFA Women's World Cup was held in 1991. The Korean women's national team has also qualified for the World Cup

Rules

Game Play Rules

How a soccer match unfolds

⏱️ Match Duration

Two halves of 45 minutes each, 90 minutes total (regulation). 15-minute halftime break. School PE classes may shorten to 20 min x 2.

🏁 Kickoff

Starts from the center circle. The first half is decided by coin toss; teams switch sides for the second half. After a goal, the team that conceded takes the kickoff.

⚽ Goal Decision

The entire ball must completely cross the goal line for a goal! If only partially over, it's no goal. Professional matches use VAR (Video Assistant Referee) to verify.

🔄 Substitutions

Regulation matches allow up to 5 substitutions (in 3 windows). In school PE, use free substitutions so everyone participates!

⏳ Added Time

Time wasted due to injuries, substitutions, VAR, etc. is added on. The referee decides and it's displayed on the scoreboard.

🎯 Penalty Shootout

In knockout matches with a tie: 30 minutes of extra time → still tied → 5 players from each team take alternating penalty kicks!

Rules · Key

The Offside Rule

The most important and most confusing rule in soccer!

  • 1
    All 3 conditions must be met for offside — (1) In the opponent's half, (2) closer to the goal than the 2nd-to-last defender, and (3) involved in the play (touch, interference, or gaining advantage)
  • 2
    Exceptions (not offside) — Receiving from a goal kick, throw-in, or corner kick / Level with or behind the ball / In your own half
Rules · Fouls

Key Fouls and Penalties

Know the fouls and you'll see the game differently!

🦵 Direct Free Kick

For pushing, tripping, kicking, holding, or handball (touching the ball with hands). Can shoot directly at goal from the spot of the foul!

🚫 Indirect Free Kick

For dangerous play (high kick), obstruction (blocking without the ball), offside, etc. Must pass once before shooting.

🟨 Yellow Card (Caution)

Repeated fouls, time-wasting, dissent, taunting, unsportsmanlike behavior. 2 yellows in one match = ejection!

🟥 Red Card (Ejection)

Violent conduct, deliberate handball or foul denying a clear goal-scoring opportunity, spitting, etc. Immediate ejection, no replacement!

🎯 Penalty Kick (PK)

When the defending team commits a direct free kick foul inside the penalty area → 1-on-1 with the GK from 11m!

📋 School PE Reminders

No slide tackles, avoid rough physical play, respect the referee's decisions. A safe and fun game is the top priority!

Tactics

Positions and Formations

11 players divide roles for organized play

  • 🧤
    GK (Goalkeeper) — Guards the goal, the only player allowed to use hands. The last line of defense!
  • 🛡️
    DF (Defenders) — CB (Center back): Blocks in front of goal / SB (Side back): Defends the flanks + supports attacks
  • ⚙️
    MF (Midfielders) — CM (Central): Links offense and defense / DM (Defensive): First line against attacks / AM (Attacking): Key passes
  • 🎯
    FW (Forwards) — ST (Striker): The goal hunter / WG (Winger): Cuts in from the flanks + crosses
  • 📐
    4-4-2 — The most balanced traditional formation. 4 defenders + 4 midfielders + 2 forwards
  • 📐
    4-3-3 — Attacking formation. Favored by Barcelona, Liverpool, and others
Core Skill 1

Kicking

Which part of the foot, which part of the ball — that's the key!

Accurate kicking starts with proper plant-foot placement + locked ankle
  • 1
    Inside Kick (instep of foot) — Maximum accuracy! Used for short passes and finishes. Lock the ankle at a right angle and push through with the area below the ankle bone
  • 2
    Instep Kick (top of foot) — Maximum power! Used for powerful shots and long passes. Extend the toes and strike the center of the ball with the middle of the instep
  • 3
    Outside Kick (outside of foot) — Creates curve! Used for bending passes and free kicks. Wrap the outside of the foot around the inside of the ball
  • 4
    Volley Kick — Striking the ball directly while it's in the air! Timing and body balance are key. Difficult but powerful

Key technique: Place plant foot 15–20cm beside the ball → Swing knee over the ball → Watch the ball at the moment of contact → Follow through (toe points toward target)

Core Skill 2

Dribbling

Keeping the ball close to your feet while moving it where you want

The key is looking up to scan the field while dribbling
  • 1
    Inside Dribble — Touch the ball with the inside of the foot while moving. Used for precise control in tight spaces
  • 2
    Outside Dribble — Push the ball with the outside of the foot. Great for quick direction changes and maintaining speed
  • 3
    Pull Away — Intentionally draw a defender in, then suddenly accelerate in the opposite direction! A space-creating technique
  • 4
    Stepover/Scissors (Feint) — Swing your foot over the ball to fake a direction change. Pretend to go one way → go the other!

Tip: Always keep the ball within one stride! Look up to maintain your field vision; kicking too hard sends the ball away and gives it up.

Core Skill 3

Passing and Trapping

Soccer is a team game! Pass accurately, receive smoothly

A good pass is one that's comfortable for the receiver
  • 1
    Short Pass (Inside) — Accurate pass to a nearby teammate using the inside of the foot. The most fundamental skill!
  • 2
    Long Pass (Instep) — Pass to a distant teammate using the top of the foot. Strike slightly below the ball to loft it
  • 3
    Through Pass — Thread the ball through gaps between defenders. Play it ahead into the attacker's running path! The most dangerous pass
  • 4
    Cross — A lofted pass from the wing into the center (in front of goal). Sets up headers!
  • 5
    Trapping (Ball Control) — Receiving the ball softly and settling it at your feet. Use sole, inside of foot, thigh, or chest

Trapping secret: At the moment of contact, slightly withdraw the body part (cushion). A stiff touch sends the ball bouncing away!

Tactics

Basic Tactics

Individual skills + team tactics = the key to victory

🔨 Build-Up Play

Short passes starting from the back (GK, defenders) to gradually advance the attack. Creates space by evading the opponent's press.

⚡ Counter-Attack

After winning the ball in defense, transition quickly! A few players sprint into open space at full speed. Speed is everything!

🔥 Pressing

Multiple players press high in the opponent's half to force passing errors. Physically demanding but highly effective.

🏃 Overlapping Run

The fullback runs past the winger on the outside during attacks to create numerical superiority. A must-have tactic in modern soccer!

🎯 Set Pieces

Pre-planned movements during corners and free kicks to create goal-scoring chances. Practice makes them a powerful weapon!

🔄 Position Rotation

Players don't stay in fixed positions but swap positions to confuse the opponent's defense.

Safety

Safety Guidelines

Stay injury-free to enjoy soccer for a long time!

🦶 Ankle & Knee Protection

Quick direction changes and tackles risk ankle sprains and knee ligament injuries! Thorough stretching + strength training exercises are essential.

💥 Collision Awareness

During headers, tense your neck muscles and keep your eyes open. Watch out for goalpost collisions! Slide tackles are strictly forbidden in school PE.

☀️ Heat Stroke Prevention

During summer outdoor exercise, hydrate every 15–20 minutes and rest in the shade. Stop immediately if you feel dizzy or nauseous!

✅ Essential Equipment

Shin guards — wear them always! Tie soccer cleats tightly, bring plenty of water, warm up for at least 10 minutes.

Remember: Even professional players don't play without protective gear! Shin guards are soccer's seatbelt.

Quiz

True/False Quiz

Let's check what you've learned!

  • Q1
    In soccer, the goalkeeper can use their hands anywhere on the field. — False. The goalkeeper can only use their hands inside their own penalty area.
  • Q2
    Offside does not apply during goal kicks, throw-ins, or corner kicks. — True. These three situations are exceptions to the offside rule.
  • Q3
    Receiving two yellow cards in one match results in ejection. — True. Two accumulated yellow cards equal a red card, resulting in ejection.
  • Q4
    A goal is awarded even if only half the ball crosses the goal line. — False. The entire ball must completely cross the goal line for a goal to count.
  • Q5
    FIFA officially recognizes medieval English ball games as the origin of soccer. — False. FIFA officially recognizes ancient Chinese Cuju as the origin of soccer.
