Class Safety Guide

The essential guide to safe PE classes

Introduction

Why Is Safety Important?

PE class is fun, but without safety, it can lead to serious accidents

  • 1
    School Accident Statistics — About 60% of school accidents occur during PE classes and physical activities. Over 100,000 school accidents are reported each year.
  • 2
    Most Are Preventable — More than 80% of PE class accidents can be prevented through proper preparation and adherence to safety rules. Small habits make a big difference.
  • 3
    Safety = Enjoyable Class — In a safe environment, students participate more actively and improve faster. When there is no fear of injury, you can focus entirely on the activity.
60% of school accidents during PE activities 80%+ are preventable Safety habits are the first step to skill improvement
Preparation

Clothing & Supplies

A checklist of clothing and supplies to check before every PE class

  • 1
    Athletic Clothing — Wear comfortable PE clothes that allow free movement. Jeans, skirts, and anything that restricts movement are strictly prohibited! Dress appropriately for the season.
  • 2
    Athletic Shoes — Wear shoes with non-slip soles. Slippers, dress shoes, and height-boosting insoles increase injury risk. Tie your laces tight!
  • 3
    Secure Glasses — Students who wear glasses should use a strap or sports band to prevent glasses from falling off or breaking.
  • 4
    Remove Jewelry — Remove all necklaces, rings, earrings, watches, and other accessories. They can snag or get caught, injuring you or others.
  • 5
    Water Bottle & Towel — Bring a personal water bottle (500 mL+) and a towel. Hydration and sweat management are fundamental to safety.

Trim your nails short too! Long nails are a hazard to both yourself and your classmates.

Warm-Up

Warm-Up

A 10-minute investment before exercise prevents injuries

  • 1
    Why It's Needed — Raises muscle temperature by 1–2°C, increasing muscle fiber elasticity. Promotes synovial fluid production in joints and gradually raises heart rate to reduce cardiac stress.
  • 2
    Dynamic Stretching Routine (10 min) — Walking in place (2 min) → Arm circles (1 min) → High knees (1 min) → Ankle circles (1 min) → Walking lunges (1 min) → Side steps (1 min) → Light jog (2 min) → Full-body shakeout (1 min)
  • 3
    Sport-Specific Warm-Up — Ball sports: include ball-handling / Running: gradually increase speed / Gymnastics: maximize joint range of motion / Swimming: focus on shoulder and ankle stretching
Muscle temp rises 1–2°C Dynamic stretching for 10 min Save static stretching for after exercise!
Cool-Down

Cool-Down

5 minutes after exercise to safely return your body to normal

  • 1
    Why It's Needed — Removes lactic acid and metabolic byproducts built up during exercise. Stopping abruptly can cause blood to pool in the legs, leading to dizziness. Gradually returns heart rate to normal.
  • 2
    Static Stretching Routine (5 min) — Light walking (1 min) → Quadriceps stretch (30 sec) → Hamstring stretch (30 sec) → Calf stretch (30 sec) → Shoulder/arm stretch (30 sec) → Lower back stretch (30 sec) → Neck stretch (30 sec) → Full-body relaxation (30 sec)
  • 3
    Tips for Each Area — Do not bounce; stretch slowly and hold for 15–30 seconds. Only stretch to the point of feeling a pull, not pain. Breathe naturally throughout.

Skipping the cool-down leads to worse muscle soreness (DOMS) the next day and decreased flexibility!

Hydration

Hydration

Water is your body's coolant — hydration management during exercise IS safety management

  • 1
    Before Exercise — Drink about 250 mL (1.5 paper cups) 30 minutes before starting. Pre-hydrating prevents dehydration during exercise.
  • 2
    During Exercise — Drink small amounts (150–200 mL) every 15–20 minutes. Drinking too much at once burdens the stomach. Drink before you feel thirsty!
  • 3
    After Exercise — Replenish the fluids lost during exercise. Drink 1.5 times the amount of weight lost, spread over 2 hours.
  • 4
    Sports Drinks vs Water — For regular classes under 1 hour, water is sufficient. Consider sports drinks for electrolyte replacement only during high-intensity exercise lasting over 1 hour.
  • 5
    Signs of Dehydration — Thirst, dry lips, dizziness, headache, dark-colored urine, increased fatigue. If you notice these signs, stop immediately and drink fluids!
Environmental Safety

Weather Precautions

Safety rules change with weather conditions

🔥 Extreme Heat

• WBGT (Wet Bulb Globe Temperature) 31°C+: No outdoor activity
• Heat stroke prevention: Wear a hat, use shade, hydrate
• Symptoms: Headache, nausea, temp 40°C+ → Call 119 immediately
• Rest every 10–15 min, move to cool area

❄️ Extreme Cold

• Hypothermia: Body temp below 35°C, shivering/confusion → Insulate
• Frostbite: Watch ears, nose, fingers, toes
• Layer up: Base layer (moisture-wicking) + Insulation + Windproof shell
• Allow 5 extra minutes for indoor warm-up

😷 Fine Dust

• "Unhealthy" level (81 μg/m³+): Limit outdoor exercise
• "Very Unhealthy" (151 μg/m³+): Switch to indoor activities
• Sensitive groups (asthma, etc.): Caution even at "Moderate"
• Build a habit of checking air quality apps in real time

🌧️ Rain / Lightning

• Rainy days: Slippery outdoor surfaces → Switch to indoors
• Lightning: Thunder within 30 sec of flash → Evacuate indoors immediately
• Stay indoors for 30 min after last lightning
• Alternatives: Indoor exercises, stretching, theory class

Facility Safety

Indoor Gym Safety

Safety rules to follow in the gymnasium

  • 1
    Check Floor Condition — Before class, check for moisture, dust, and debris on the floor. Slippery floors are a leading cause of falls.
  • 2
    Equipment Placement — Store unused equipment along the walls. Obstacles in the activity area create collision and tripping hazards.
  • 3
    Mat Inspection — For gymnastics and vault classes, always check mat seams and thickness. Ensure mats have non-slip backing so they don't shift.
  • 4
    Ventilation — Vigorous exercise in an enclosed gym can lead to oxygen depletion and heat buildup. Open windows or activate ventilation systems to ensure air circulation.
  • 5
    Lighting & Doorways — Never exercise in poorly lit areas. Doorway zones are collision risks — keep them outside the activity perimeter.
Facility Safety

Outdoor Field Safety

Safety points to check on the outdoor field

  • 1
    Ground Condition — Before class, check for rocks, glass shards, and uneven patches. After rain, watch especially for mud and puddles.
  • 2
    Drain / Manhole Covers — Ensure drain covers are securely fixed. A loose or shifted cover can cause a foot to fall through, resulting in serious injury.
  • 3
    Sun Protection — During summer outdoor activities, apply sunscreen (SPF 30+) and wear a hat. Sunglasses are also recommended for eye protection.
  • 4
    Boundary Lines — Clearly mark the activity zone and maintain a safe distance (at least 2 m) from walls, pillars, and fences beyond the boundary.
  • 5
    Shared Space Awareness — When sharing the field with other classes or the public, clearly separate activity zones and consider the direction balls may travel.
Equipment Safety

Equipment Safety

Proper management and use of equipment is key to injury prevention

  • 1
    Check Ball Pressure — Under-inflated balls increase the risk of wrist/finger injuries, while over-inflated balls deliver excessive impact. Check for proper inflation before class.
  • 2
    Secure Nets & Goals — Verify that volleyball nets, soccer goals, handball goals, etc. are firmly secured. A toppling goal can cause serious accidents.
  • 3
    Wear Protective Gear — Depending on the sport, wear the required gear: helmets (baseball batting), chest protectors (fencing), knee pads, shin guards (soccer), etc.
  • 4
    Report Damaged Equipment — If you find cracked or broken equipment, report it to the teacher immediately and stop using it. "It'll be fine" is the most dangerous attitude!

Putting equipment back in its proper place after use is also part of safety. Abandoned equipment is a hazard to others.

Emergency Response

What to Do When an Accident Occurs

If an accident happens, stay calm and act step by step

  • ①
    Stop All Activity — When an accident occurs, all students stop activity and secure the area around the injured person. Preventing further accidents is the top priority!
  • ②
    Assess the Injured Person — Check consciousness, breathing, bleeding, and for possible fractures. Do NOT move the person carelessly. Especially if neck/back injury is suspected, do NOT move them at all.
  • ③
    Call 119 — For serious injuries (unconsciousness, heavy bleeding, suspected fracture), call 119 immediately. Communicate the exact location and situation.
  • ④
    Administer First Aid — For minor injuries, follow the RICE protocol (Rest–Ice–Compression–Elevation). For heavy bleeding, apply direct pressure with a clean cloth.
  • ⑤
    Contact Guardian / Homeroom Teacher — Regardless of severity, contact the homeroom teacher and the student's guardian. Always report even minor injuries.
  • ⑥
    Write an Accident Report — Record the date, location, circumstances, injury severity, and treatment given. This is required for school safety insurance claims.
Quiz

True / False Quiz

Let's check what you have learned!

  • Q1
    Static stretching should be done first before PE class. — False. Dynamic stretching is more effective before exercise. Static stretching is suited for the cool-down afterwards.
  • Q2
    During exercise, you should drink water before you feel thirsty. — True. By the time you feel thirsty, your body is already 1–2% dehydrated. Drink every 15–20 minutes proactively.
  • Q3
    At a "Moderate" fine dust level, outdoor PE can proceed freely. — False. Students with respiratory conditions like asthma in the sensitive group still need caution at "Moderate" levels.
  • Q4
    Stopping exercise abruptly can cause dizziness. — True. Sudden stops cause blood to pool in the legs, reducing blood flow to the brain and potentially causing dizziness.
  • Q5
    A person who injured their neck or back should be helped up quickly. — False. If a neck or back injury is suspected, never move the person. Maintain them in a fixed position until 119 paramedics arrive.
Key Summary

Remember This!

Key points for safe PE classes

📌 Before Class

• Wear PE clothes & shoes, remove jewelry
• 10-min dynamic stretching warm-up
• Prepare water bottle & towel, pre-hydrate
• Check weather, facilities, and equipment

⭐ During Class

• Hydrate every 15–20 min
• Indoor: Check floor, mats, ventilation
• Outdoor: Check ground, drains, UV exposure
• Use equipment properly, report damage immediately

🚨 Accident Response

• Stop activity → Assess condition → Call 119
• RICE protocol (Rest · Ice · Compression · Elevation)
• Never move a suspected neck/back injury
• Write an accident report
