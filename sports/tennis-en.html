Introduction

What is Tennis?

The crown jewel of racket sports — hit the ball over a net with a racket

  • 1
    Net Competition Sport — Hit a tennis ball across the net, returning it within 1 bounce. 2 bounces = point lost.
  • 2
    Singles and Doubles — 1v1 singles and 2v2 doubles. Doubles uses the wider court including the alleys.
  • 3
    4 Grand Slams — Australian Open (Jan, hard court), French Open (May-Jun, clay), Wimbledon (Jun-Jul, grass), US Open (Aug-Sep, hard). Winning all 4 = 'Career Grand Slam.'
  • 4
    Korean Tennis Pride — Chung Hyeon (2018 Australian Open semifinal), Kwon Soon-woo (Korea's highest-ever men's ranking) and others are raising interest in tennis.

History

History of Tennis

The king of racket sports, born in 12th-century French monasteries

  • 1
    12th Century: French 'Jeu de Paume' — Tennis originated from 'Jeu de Paume,' a ball-hitting game played with palms in French monasteries. 'Tennis' comes from French 'Tenez' (meaning 'Take this!').
  • 2
    1877: First Wimbledon — The first official tournament at the All England Club in London. Wimbledon remains the oldest and most prestigious tennis event.
  • 3
    1896: Early Modern Olympics — Part of the first modern Athens Olympics. Dropped in 1924, returned at the 1988 Seoul Olympics.
  • 4
    Open Era (1968~) — From 1968, professionals could compete in Grand Slams, ushering in modern tennis. Before that, only amateurs were allowed.
  • 5
    Korean Tennis (1908~) — Tennis was introduced to Korea by missionaries in 1908. Players like Lee Hyung-taik and Chung Hyeon elevated Korean tennis on the world stage.
  • 6
    Court Types — Hard court (fast, Australian/US Open), clay (slow, high bounce, French Open), grass (fastest, Wimbledon). Each surface demands different tactics.

Rules · Scoring

Tennis Scoring System

Understanding the unique scoring makes tennis 10x more fun!

  • 1
    Point → Game → Set → Match — Collect points to win a game, games to win a set, sets to win the match.
  • 2
    Unique Score Reading — 0=Love, 1pt=15, 2pts=30, 3pts=40, 4pts=Game. E.g., Server 2pts, Receiver 1pt = "30-15."
  • 3
    Deuce and Advantage — At 40-40, it's 'Deuce.' Win 1 point = 'Advantage'; win another = Game. Must lead by 2 consecutive points.
  • 4
    Set — Win 6 games to take a set. At 6-5, must win 7-5; at 6-6, a tiebreak (first to 7, lead by 2) decides.
  • 5
    Match — Most events: best of 3 sets (win 2). Men's Grand Slams only: best of 5 sets (win 3).
  • 6
    Score Reading Example — "Game, Federer. Federer leads 5-4, first set." Server's score is read first!
Remember: The 15-30-40 progression likely comes from a clock face: 1/4 (15 min), 2/4 (30 min), 3/4 (45→40).
Rules · Match

Match Rules

From serves to challenges — core tennis rules

↗️ Serve Rules

Serve diagonally. 2 chances: if the first fails (fault), take a second serve. Both fail = double fault (point lost).

🔄 Let

If a serve hits the net and lands in the service court, it's a 'let' — re-serve. Unlimited repeats.

✅ In / Out

If the ball touches the line at all, it's in! Must land completely outside the line for out. "Touching the line = in."

🔁 Court Change

Switch sides whenever the sum of games is odd (1-0, 2-1, 3-2...). Equalizes sun and wind effects.

📺 Challenge (Hawk-Eye)

Players can challenge line calls. Hawk-Eye (video review) precisely determines in/out. Up to 3 per set.

👥 Doubles Rules

Court extends to the alleys. Serve order rotates among 4 players. After the return, either partner can hit.

Core Skill 1

Serve

The start of the game and the most powerful weapon

  • 1
    Flat Serve — Minimal spin, straight and fast. Maximum speed; used as a first serve to aim for aces.
  • 2
    Slice Serve — Sidespin that curves after bouncing. Pushes the opponent wide to create open court.
  • 3
    Kick Serve — Heavy topspin that bounces high. Reliable second serve option.
  • 4
    Serve Sequence — Toss → Trophy pose (knees bend, racket back) → Swing (strike at peak) → Follow-through. The only self-initiated shot — practice it the most!
Core Skill 2

Groundstrokes

Basic offense and defense from the baseline

  • 1
    Forehand — Hit balls on your racket arm's side. Most players' strongest weapon.
  • 2
    Backhand — Hit balls on the opposite side. Two-handed (stable, powerful) or one-handed (longer reach, elegant slice).
  • 3
    Topspin — Swing low to high for forward spin. The ball rises then dips sharply, allowing powerful shots that stay in.
  • 4
    Slice — Cut high to low for backspin. The ball skids low, stealing the opponent's timing.
  • 5
    Movement Flow — Split step → footwork → takeback → swing → follow-through → recovery. Feet move first for good shots!
Core Skill 3

Volleys and Special Shots

Net finishing power and diverse weapons

  • 1
    Forehand Volley — Hit without a bounce near the net. Short, punchy swing gives the opponent no time to react.
  • 2
    Backhand Volley — One-handed no-bounce hit on the opposite side. Precise racket face control is key.
  • 3
    Smash (Overhead) — Slam a high lob down hard from above. Similar to serve motion; almost always a winner.
  • 4
    Drop Shot — From a power swing stance, suddenly soften the touch to drop the ball just over the net. Effective when the opponent is far back.
  • 5
    Lob — A high shot over a net-rushing opponent. Defensive (time-buying) and offensive (winner-seeking) varieties.
  • 6
    Approach Shot — Hit a short ball while advancing to the net, setting up a volley or smash finish.
Tactics

Tennis Tactics

Skill isn't enough — play chess on the court!

🧱 Baseline Play

Rally from the back with groundstrokes, forcing errors or creating opportunities. The most common modern style.

⚡ Serve and Volley

Hit a strong serve then rush the net to finish with a volley. Especially effective on grass courts.

↔️ Court Utilization

Send balls to corners side-to-side, plus drop shots and lobs front-to-back. Create openings for winners!

🎯 Exploit Weaknesses

Most backhands are weaker — concentrate there. Against tired players, go wide; against poor net players, use drop shots.

🛡️ Consistency

"Return one more ball than your opponent." Steady, deep returns that force opponent errors. Most important for students!

📊 Service vs Return Game

Hold your serve game (advantage); break the opponent's serve game to lead the set.

Safety Guidelines

Playing Safely

Essential safety tips for injury prevention

💪 Tennis Elbow

Repetitive impact on the outer elbow tendons. Prevent with proper grip, appropriate racket weight, and elbow stretching.

🦵 Shoulder Injuries

Repeated serves/overheads strain the rotator cuff. Gradually increase practice volume and do shoulder strengthening.

🦶 Ankle & Knee Injuries

Frequent stops and direction changes load ankle and knee ligaments. Tennis shoes and proper footwork are key.

☀️ Heat & UV

Summer outdoor play risks heat stroke! Wear hats, apply sunscreen, hydrate at every changeover, rest in shade.

💧 Hydration

Drink water or sports drinks at every court change (~90 sec). Drink before you feel thirsty!

🤸 Stretching!

Shoulders, elbows, wrists, back, knees, ankles in order. Cool down after to relieve fatigue.

Fun Facts

Tennis Trivia

Learn more about tennis!

  • Q1
    Why is 0 called 'Love'? — Likely from French 'l'oeuf' (egg), which sounds like 'love.' An egg shape resembles 0!
  • Q2
    Wimbledon traditions? — Players must wear white; spectators eat strawberries and cream. The only Grand Slam on grass.
  • Q3
    Why are tennis balls yellow? — White until 1972, then changed to fluorescent yellow (optic yellow) for better TV visibility.
  • Q4
    Longest match ever? — 2010 Wimbledon: Isner vs Mahut, 11 hours 5 minutes over 3 days! Final set: 70-68.
  • Q5
    GOAT debate — Federer (elegance), Nadal (clay king), Djokovic (most Grand Slams) — the greatest of all time debate burns hot.

Quiz

True/False Quiz

Let's check what you've learned!

  • Q1
    Tennis scores go 0, 15, 30, 40. — True. The unique scoring system; 0 is called 'love.'
  • Q2
    You can serve up to 3 times per point. — False. Maximum 2 (first and second serve). Both fail = double fault.
  • Q3
    Wimbledon is played on clay courts. — False. Wimbledon is on grass. Clay is the French Open (Roland Garros).
  • Q4
    Deuce means 40:40. — True. After deuce, you must win 2 consecutive points for the game.
  • Q5
    Doubles uses a narrower court than singles. — False. Doubles uses the alleys, making the court wider.
