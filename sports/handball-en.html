Handball

Throw with your hands, block with your body — the most dynamic ball sport

Introduction

What is Handball?

A 7-a-side territorial competition sport — score by throwing the ball into the opponent's goal

  • 1
    Objective — Pass and dribble the ball by hand, then throw it into the opponent's goal (3m x 2m) to score more points
  • 2
    Players — 7 players per team on the court including 1 goalkeeper (up to 7 substitutes)
  • 3
    Characteristics — A hybrid sport combining soccer's goal attacks, basketball's fast passing, and water polo's throwing elements
  • 4
    Global reach — Especially popular in Europe; an Olympic sport enjoyed in over 180 countries worldwide
Olympic official sport 7-a-side indoor game Soccer + basketball + water polo combined Top 5 popular sport in Europe
History

History of Handball

A dynamic sport born in Germany that spread across the world

  • 1917
    Handball is born — German PE teacher Max Heiser created it as a sport for women. It was originally an outdoor field game with 11 players per side.
  • 1936
    Olympic debut — 11-a-side outdoor handball was included at the Berlin Olympics. Host nation Germany won the gold medal.
  • 1972
    Indoor handball era — The current 7-a-side indoor format returned at the Munich Olympics. The game evolved to become faster and more dynamic!
  • 1988
    Korean women's handball gold — The Korean women's team won gold at the Seoul Olympics! The beginning of Korea as a handball powerhouse.
  • 1992
    Back-to-back gold — The women's team won gold again at the Barcelona Olympics! An incredible record of two consecutive Olympic golds.

Did you know? Korean women's handball has won 2 gold and 2 silver Olympic medals. Their fighting spirit in defeating larger European teams despite their smaller stature is a proud chapter in Korean sports history!

Rules · Game Play

Game Play Rules

Here's how a handball game is played

⏱️ Two 30-Minute Halves

A regulation game: first half 30 min + second half 30 min (60 min total). School PE may shorten to 15 min per half. 10-minute halftime break.

🏁 Center Throw Start

The game starts and restarts after goals with a center throw! One foot on the center line, pass at the whistle to begin.

🚶 3-Step Rule

After catching the ball, you can take a maximum of 3 steps! You must pass, shoot, or dribble within those steps. 4+ steps = overstep violation.

⏳ 3-Second Rule

After catching the ball, you must pass, shoot, or dribble within 3 seconds. Stalling is a violation!

🏀 Dribbling Allowed

You can bounce the ball while moving (similar to basketball). But once you pick it up again, the 3-step and 3-second rules apply!

🚫 No Entering Goal Area

Field players cannot enter the goal area (6m semicircle). Only the goalkeeper may be in this zone!

Rules · Fouls & Penalties

Key Fouls

Rules for fair and safe play

🚶 Overstep

Taking 4+ steps while holding the ball is a violation. You must pass, shoot, or dribble within 3 steps!

⏳ Holding Too Long

Holding the ball for more than 3 seconds is a violation. Quick decisions are essential.

💥 Physical Foul

Pushing, grabbing, hitting, or rough contact is a violation. Defend with your body, but only fairly!

🚫 Goal Area Violation

A field player entering the goal area is a violation. Landing there after an aerial shot also counts!

⚽ Free Throw

For general fouls, a free throw is taken from the foul spot or the 9m line. Defenders must stay 3m+ away.

🎯 7m Throw (Penalty)

Awarded when a clear scoring chance is denied by a foul. 1-on-1 with the GK — high scoring chance.

🟡 Warning (Yellow Card)

For repeated or rough fouls. 1 per player, up to 3 per team.

🔴 Ejection & 2-Min Suspension

Serious fouls = 2-minute suspension (playing short-handed); violent behavior = immediate red card! Team plays at numerical disadvantage.

Core Skill 1

Shooting

The most important skill for scoring — the wrist snap is key!

  • 1
    Standing Shot — Basic shot with both feet on the ground. The starting point for learning form and wrist snap.
  • 2
    Jump Shot — The most iconic shot, thrown while jumping. Shoot from a high point over defensive blocks.
  • 3
    Spin Shot — Advanced technique of throwing while spinning. Deceives defenders and creates angles.
  • 4
    Falling Shot — Shot thrown while falling forward. Gets as close to goal as possible from the goal area edge.

Key point: Every shot finishes with a wrist snap! Use a quick wrist snap to add spin and speed — making it harder for the goalkeeper to react.

Core Skill 2

Passing

Fast, accurate connections are the lifeblood of handball!

  • 1
    Overhead Pass — The most basic and commonly used pass, thrown with one hand from above the head. Accurate and long-range.
  • 2
    Bounce Pass — Bounced off the floor to get under defenders' arms. Commonly used to connect with the pivot player.
  • 3
    Sidearm Pass — Thrown with the arm extended sideways for sharp passes that zip past defenders.
  • 4
    Back Pass — An advanced pass thrown backward without looking. Confuses the defense with unexpected direction changes.

Key point: You must pass within 3 seconds, so quick linking passes are the core of offense. Decide your next play the moment you receive!

Core Skill 3

Defense

Strong defense creates victories!

  • 1
    6-0 Defense — The most basic formation: 6 players in a line in front of the goal area. Creates a tight wall to reduce shooting angles.
  • 2
    5-1 Defense — 5 players in back, 1 pushed forward to pressure the opponent's center back and disrupt passing.
  • 3
    Blocking — Raise both arms high to block the opponent's shot! Requires courage and timing.
  • 4
    Interception — Stealing an opponent's pass mid-flight. Success leads directly to a fast break!

Goalkeeper: The last line of defense! Requires excellent reflexes and courage, throwing their entire body to block shots. A single great save can change the game.

Tactics · Positions

Positions

7 players complete the team with their individual roles

  • CB
    Center Back (Playmaker) — The team's field general! Directs the offense and orchestrates passes. Requires excellent vision and judgment.
  • LB·RB
    Left Back · Right Back — Wing attackers. The team's main scorers with powerful long-range shooting and penetration.
  • PV
    Pivot (Line Player) — Operates between defenders for screens and central breakthroughs. Strong physicality is a must!
  • LW·RW
    Left Wing · Right Wing — Fast breaks and sharp-angle shots from the edges. Speed and agility are essential.
  • GK
    Goalkeeper — The ultimate guardian. Requires reflexes, courage, and precise positioning.
Tactics

Basic Tactics

Individual skills + team tactics determine the outcome

⚡ Fast Break

Attack quickly after winning the ball before the opponent's defense recovers! The goalkeeper's accurate first pass starts the fast break. The most efficient scoring method.

🔄 Set Play

When fast break isn't possible, switch to positional attack! Move the ball quickly to create gaps. Patience and teamwork are key.

✂️ Cross (Position Swap)

Two players cross paths to confuse the defense. Target the moment when a defender loses their mark.

🧱 Screen

The pivot stands like a wall in front of a defender to create shooting space. Similar to a basketball screen.

🏃 Numerical Advantage

When an opponent gets a 2-minute suspension, exploit the advantage! Use extra space to pass and attack open areas. Best scoring opportunity!

Safety Guidelines

Staying Safe While Playing

Enjoying the game injury-free is the most important thing!

💪 Shoulder Injury Risk

Powerful shooting puts great stress on the shoulder. Thorough stretching and gradual practice are essential! Stop immediately if you feel pain.

🖐️ Finger & Wrist Protection

Fingers and wrists are easily injured when catching or blocking. Spread fingers when receiving; wrist guards are recommended.

💥 Collision Awareness

Moving quickly in tight spaces increases collision risk. Avoid excessive contact and always watch your surroundings.

🦵 Knee Protection

Repeated jump shots stress the knees. Bend knees when landing; knee pads are recommended.

✅ Warm-Up Required!

At least 10 minutes: shoulder rotations, wrist stretches, knee bends, light jogging.

🧤 Equipment Check

Tie shoes tightly, trim nails, prepare water, wear knee/wrist guards if needed. Safety first!

Practice Activities

Try It in Class

Build your handball skills with step-by-step practice

  • 1
    Catch Ball Relay — Stand in two lines facing each other and practice overhead passes. Work on accurate catches and quick releases. Drop it? Start over!
  • 2
    Shooting Practice — Practice from the 9m line: standing shot then jump shot. Aim at the four corners of the goal to improve accuracy.
  • 3
    3v3 Mini Game — Split the court for 3-on-3 matches. Develop quick decision-making and passing in a smaller space.
  • 4
    Offense-Defense Drill — 3 attackers vs 2 defenders + GK. Move the ball to find open spaces and shoot!
  • 5
    Full 7v7 Game — Real game with all rules. Rotate positions and try out tactics.

Class tip: Start with a soft ball to reduce fear. Making the goal area larger helps beginners enjoy the game safely!
