Volleyball

Perfect teamwork of 6 players across a net

Introduction

What is Volleyball?

A 6-a-side net competition sport — send the ball over the net to the opponent's court within 3 touches before it hits the floor

  • 1
    Objective — Score by making the ball land on the opponent's court! If the opponent fails to return it, your team scores
  • 2
    Players — 6 per team on court + substitutes including libero. 14-player roster
  • 3
    How to play — Receive → Set → Spike! Connect the ball within 3 touches to complete an attack
  • 4
    Characteristics — An Olympic staple; in Korea, the women's V-League is enormously popular
1895 Born at a US YMCA Olympic sport (since 1964) 800+ million players worldwide 🇰🇷 Korea's V-League draws huge crowds
History

History of Volleyball

From Mintonette to Volleyball — 130 years of evolution

  • 1895
    Volleyball is born — William G. Morgan, a PE teacher at Holyoke YMCA in Massachusetts, invented it. Originally called 'Mintonette'! He wanted an indoor sport less intense than basketball.
  • 1896
    Name change — Professor Alfred Halstead, watching a demo game, saw players volleying the ball over the net and suggested renaming it 'Volleyball.'
  • 1964
    Olympic debut — Men's and women's volleyball became official sports at the Tokyo Olympics. Japan's women's team won gold, earning the nickname 'Witches of the Orient!'
  • 2005
    Korea's V-League launches — Korea's professional V-League began. Stars like Kim Yeon-koung emerged, fueling a volleyball boom.
  • 2021
    Volleyball fever — Korea's women's team reached the semifinals at the Tokyo Olympics, sparking nationwide volleyball enthusiasm!

Fun fact! Morgan was a junior colleague of Naismith, who invented basketball. Both came from the YMCA!

Rules · Game Play

Game Rules

Set system + rally point — intense close matches

🏆 Best of 5 Sets

Win 3 out of 5 sets to win the match. Each set is won at 25 points (must lead by 2). Set 5 (deciding set) is played to 15 points.

⚡ Rally Point System

Every rally earns a point! The team that wins the rally gets 1 point regardless of who served.

🔄 6-Player Rotation

When your team regains serve, all players rotate one position clockwise! Every player experiences both front and back row.

✋ 3 Touches Max

A team can touch the ball a maximum of 3 times! Usually: Receive (1) → Set (2) → Spike (3). Block touches don't count.

🚫 Double Contact

One player touching the ball twice in a row is a violation! However, touching after a block by the same player is allowed.

⚠️ Net Touch Violation

Touching the net during play is a violation! Be careful not to touch it during spikes or blocks. Carrying (holding) is also illegal.

Positions

Key Positions

Individual roles combine to form one team

  • S
    Setter — The team's quarterback! Delivers optimal sets to attackers. Quick judgment and precise hand skills are essential. Called volleyball's 'point guard.'
  • OH
    Outside Hitter — The main attacker! Delivers powerful spikes from the left side. Must also participate in receiving. Kim Yeon-koung plays OH!
  • OP
    Opposite (Right Side) — Positioned diagonally from the setter. Attacks from the right side and frequently attempts back attacks.
  • MB
    Middle Blocker — The guardian of center net! Quick attacks and blocking are the main duties. Requires fast reactions and high jumping ability.
  • L
    Libero — Defensive specialist! Wears a different colored jersey and plays only in the back row. Can substitute freely but cannot attack (spike in front of attack line) or serve.

Rotation rule: Players rotate clockwise by serve order, but after the serve they move to their specialized positions. This is called 'position switching!'

Core Skill 1

Serve

Every rally begins with a serve

  • 1
    Underhand Serve — Hit upward with a fist from below. The best serve for beginners to learn first.
  • 2
    Overhand Serve — Strike the ball with your palm from above. Relatively easy to control direction and power.
  • 3
    Float Serve — A no-spin serve that wobbles unpredictably. Hard to receive, widely used by pros.
  • 4
    Jump Serve — Run up, jump, and slam down like a spike. Maximum power but high error rate!

Service ace — When a serve directly scores! The best servers can change the flow of a match.

Core Skill 2

Receive (Pass)

Good attacks start with accurate receives

  • 1
    Underhand Pass (Bump) — Receive with a flat platform formed by both forearms. Essential for receiving powerful serves and spikes!
  • 2
    Overhand Pass (Set) — Push the ball with fingers of both hands from above the forehead. Used by setters to deliver accurate sets to attackers.
  • 3
    Dig — A low-stance defensive technique to receive opponent spikes. Requires quick reactions and bold body movement!

3 steps of receiving: Move feet (to the ball's path) → Get in position (low, form arm platform) → Impact (control direction with arm angle)

Core Skill 3

Spike (Attack)

The most thrilling moment in volleyball!

  • 1
    Approach — Quick 3–4 step run toward the net. Spread your feet wide on the last two steps to prepare for the jump!
  • 2
    Jump — Jump powerfully with both feet! Swing both arms up to maximize hang time and height.
  • 3
    Hit — At the peak, slam the ball down with your palm! Draw your arm back like a bow and swing fast.
  • 4
    Follow-through — Let your arm swing naturally down and land stably. Two-foot landing is important for injury prevention.

Skills · Defense

Blocking and Defense

A wall at the net + a shield on the court

🧱 Blocking

Jump at the net to block opponent spikes! 2–3 players leap together to form a wall. Timing and positioning are key. Block touches don't count toward team touches.

🏊 Dig

Receive powerful spikes in a low stance. The libero's key ability! Save the ball with any body part before it hits the floor.

🦸 Save (Dive)

Diving to reach a ball far away. Includes the pancake (placing one hand flat on the floor for the ball to bounce off).

🤝 Cover

Backing up a teammate's attack in case the ball is blocked back. Teammates crouch low around the spiker for good coverage.

Defensive formation: Fill the gaps behind the blockers! Reading the opponent's attack direction is key.

Tactics

Tactics and Strategy

Volleyball is also a mind game

🧠 5-1 System

One setter handles all sets — the most common system. When the setter is in front row, they also attack; in back row, they focus on setting. The setter's ability drives team strength!

⚡ Quick Attack

A-quick: Fast attack right in front of setter. B-quick: 2m from setter. C-quick: Behind the setter. The middle blocker's primary weapon!

🎭 Combination Attack

The middle fakes a quick, then a different attacker hits. Steals the opposing blockers' timing, creating 1-on-1 or unmarked situations.

🚀 Pipe (Back Row Attack)

A back-row player jumps from behind the attack line to hit. When blockers focus on front-row hitters, the back-row surprise is hard to stop!

📋 Serve Receive Formation

Player positioning to efficiently receive the opponent's serve. W-shape (5 players), 3-player receive, etc. Key is protecting weak receivers.

🛡️ Blocking System

Read blocking: React after seeing the set (accurate). Commit blocking: Pre-jump on a predicted hitter (fast but risky). Chosen based on team strategy!

Safety Guidelines

Staying Safe While Playing

Enjoying the game injury-free is the most important thing!

🦶 Ankle Injuries (Most Common!)

The most common injury occurs when landing after spikes and blocks! Stepping on another player's foot or landing unevenly risks sprains. Wear ankle braces and practice two-foot landings.

🖐️ Finger Injuries

Fingers can bend back during blocking, or get hit while setting/receiving. Tape fingers together for support and keep fingers spread during blocks!

💪 Shoulder Injuries

Repeated spiking strains the rotator cuff. Shoulder stretching and strength exercises are essential! Rest immediately if you feel pain.

🦵 Knee Injuries

Diving and repeated jumping stress the knees. Knee pads are recommended; be especially careful on hard floors.

Remember! 10+ minutes warm-up (especially ankles, shoulders, fingers), taping recommended, stay well hydrated!

Appeal of Volleyball

Why Volleyball?

The unique appeal only volleyball has

  • 1
    Perfect Team Sport — You can never score alone! Receive → Set → Spike requires at least 2–3 players. The team covering for one person's mistake is the essence of volleyball.
  • 2
    No Physical Contact — Playing across a net means no collisions with opponents. Size differences aren't absolute; skill and strategy matter more!
  • 3
    Quick Transitions — With rally point scoring, every point matters! One moment of focus can turn a match around.
  • 4
    Diverse Positions — Tall? Middle blocker. Agile? Libero. Smart? Setter! Find the position that suits your strengths.
  • 5
    For Everyone — Adjust the net height and anyone can play. From school to club to pro league — a lifelong sport!
Quiz

True/False Quiz

Let's check what you've learned!

  • Q1
    In volleyball, a team can touch the ball up to 4 times. — False. Maximum 3 touches. Block touches don't count.
  • Q2
    Volleyball was originally called 'Mintonette.' — True. That was its name when William Morgan invented it in 1895, later changed to Volleyball.
  • Q3
    The libero can spike in front of the attack line. — False. The libero is a back-row defensive specialist and cannot attack from in front of the attack line.
  • Q4
    Volleyball rotation moves clockwise. — True. When your team regains serve, all players rotate one position clockwise.
  • Q5
    In official matches, all sets are played to 25 points. — False. Sets 1–4 are to 25, but set 5 (deciding set) is played to 15.
