Flying Disc

Throw, catch, and run — a team sport completed by the players' honesty, without referees. Learn the rules, skills, and tactics of Ultimate.

Introduction

What Is Flying Disc?

A non-contact team sport where you pass a disc and score by catching it in the opposing end zone

  • 1
    Objective — Advance the disc through passes and score by catching it in the opponent's end zone
  • 2
    Players — 7 per team (school classes: can be reduced to 5)
  • 3
    Core principle — You cannot run with the disc (pivot foot only)
Non-contact No physical contact Self-officiated Spirit of the Game Official mixed-gender sport
History · Origins

A Fun Origin Story

From a pie tin to a worldwide sport

  • 1
    1871 — The Frisbie Pie Company was established in Connecticut. An empty pie tin started it all!
  • 2
    1940s — Yale University students began throwing empty pie tins while shouting "Frisbie!"
  • 3
    1957 — Wham-O trademarked a plastic disc as "Frisbee" and began mass production
  • 4
    1968 — Joel Silver, a student at Columbia High School in New Jersey, created the first Ultimate rules

Why "Flying Disc" instead of "Frisbee"? — "Frisbee" is a registered trademark of Wham-O, so official sports use the generic term "Flying Disc."

History · Growth

Global Growth

Flying disc's journey toward the Olympics

🏛️ WFDF Founded (1985)

The World Flying Disc Federation was established. Currently has 80+ member countries. An IOC-recognized international sports federation.

🏅 Olympic Bid

IOC provisional recognition in 2015. Pursuing demonstration sport status at the 2028 LA Olympics. Official sport at Asian Games and Universiade.

🇰🇷 Flying Disc in Korea

The Korea Flying Disc Federation (KFDF) operates national competitions and school sports club leagues.

🌍 Diverse Disciplines

Evolved into various formats: Ultimate (team), Disc Golf, Guts, Freestyle, and more.

Field Layout

Key zones of an Ultimate field

  • 🥏

  • 👕

  • 🔻
    Equipment — Line cones (for marking field corners). Essential for school classes.
Rules · Game Start

Pull — Starting the Game

The equivalent of a kickoff in Ultimate

  • 1
    Line up — Both teams line up along their respective end zone lines
  • 2
    Ready signal — All offensive players raise one hand to signal readiness
  • 3
    Pull! — The defensive team throws the disc deep into the opposing half
  • 4
    Offense begins — The offensive team catches or picks up the disc and starts the attack from that spot

Brick rule — If the pull goes out of bounds, the offense can start from the brick mark at mid-field.

Rules · Turnovers

Turnovers (Possession Change)

When these situations occur, offense and defense switch

⬇️ Drop

A pass is not caught and the disc falls to the ground.

🚫 Out

The disc goes out of bounds.

🖐️ Intercept

A defender catches the disc in mid-air.

⏱️ Stall-out

Failure to throw the disc within 10 seconds.

🤚 Handover

The disc is handed directly to an opponent.

👋 Deflection

A defender knocks the disc down, causing it to fall.

Key! — On a turnover, the opposing team begins their offense from the exact spot where the disc hit the ground.

Rules · Fouls

Stall Count & Fouls

Time limits and foul rules in flying disc

⏱️ Stall Count

The mark (defender) counts "Stalling 1, 2, 3...10" from within 3m. You must pass within 10 seconds.

💥 Contact Foul

If a defender contacts the thrower during the throwing motion, it is a foul.

🏃 Travel

Lifting the pivot foot or taking 3+ steps is a violation.

🚧 Pick

A third player blocking a defender's path is a foul.

Call system — The fouled player calls "Foul!" directly → if the opponent agrees, play by rules; if contested, resume from the previous state. All calls are resolved through player dialogue!

Rules · Scoring

Scoring and Game Format

How to score and determine the winner

🎉 Scoring

Catch the disc inside the opponent's end zone for 1 point! After scoring, the scoring team pulls (serves).

🏁 Win Condition

First to 15 points (world competition). Cap rule: maximum 17 points.

🔄 Halftime

When a team reaches 8 points, sides are switched with a 5-minute break.

🏫 School Adaptation

Timed format (10 min x 2 quarters) or first to 5 points. 5v5 with mixed-gender teams recommended.

Key Skills 1

Backhand Throw

The basic throw with excellent accuracy and distance

  • 1
    Grip — Thumb on top of the disc, other 4 fingers curled under the rim
  • 2
    Stance — Turn your body sideways and pull the arm across your chest
  • 3
    Release — Extend the arm and use wrist snap for a level release
  • 4
    Follow-through — Extend the arm fully to keep the disc level throughout
Key Skills 2

Forehand (Sidearm)

A quick-release throw that disrupts defensive timing

  • 1
    Grip — Index and middle finger under the rim, thumb on top
  • 2
    Stance — Bend the elbow at 90° beside the body
  • 3
    Motion — Rapidly rotate the forearm around the elbow
  • 4
    Snap — Maximize spin with a wrist flick on release
Key Skills 3 & 4

Catching & Hammer Throw

Catching techniques and an advanced throw

🤲 Pancake Catch

Press down on the disc with both hands like a sandwich. The most stable method for beginners.

👏 Clap Catch

Grab both sides of the rim. Thumbs down above waist, thumbs up below waist.

🔨 Hammer Throw

Throw overhead with a forehand grip in a downward chopping motion. The disc flips and curves. Used to throw over defenders' heads.

🏊 Layout

A diving catch. The highlight of Ultimate! Slide landing chest → belly. Only attempt after sufficient practice.

Tactics · Strategy

Basic Tactics

Team strategies that change the game

  • 1
    Cut — Sprint into open space to receive a pass
  • 2
    Stack — Teammates line up in a row and break out in sequence to create space
  • 3
    Force — A defender blocks one direction to limit the thrower's options
  • 4
    Role division — Handlers (circulate passes) + cutters (run toward the end zone)
Core Values

Spirit of the Game

What makes flying disc truly special

  • ⚖️
    Self-officiated — Players make their own calls and resolve disputes through dialogue, without referees
  • 🛡️
    Safe sport — Non-contact principle means low injury risk regardless of body size
  • 👫
    Mixed gender — Mixed-gender teams are required even in official competitions. Equal participation regardless of gender
  • 🧠
    Strategic thinking — Develops quick decision-making, spatial awareness, and creative problem-solving
Safety Guidelines

Playing Safely

Staying injury-free is the most important thing

⚠️ Warm-up

Start with adequate stretching. Focus especially on shoulder, wrist, and ankle joints.

⚠️ Check Surroundings

Always check for people nearby before throwing. Never throw hard directly at someone's face.

⚠️ Collision Warning

If physical contact occurs, stop immediately and yield. Maintain at least 3m spacing.

✅ Preparation

Comfortable athletic shoes, trimmed fingernails, adequate hydration. Watch for slippery grass.

Quiz

True/False Quiz

Let's check what you've learned!

  • Q1
    In Ultimate, you can run with the disc. — False. Once you catch the disc, you must stop and can only use a pivot foot.
  • Q2
    Flying disc games have no referees. — True. Under the "Spirit of the Game," players make their own calls and resolve disputes through dialogue.
  • Q3
    You have 15 seconds to pass after catching the disc. — False. The stall count is 10 seconds; failure to pass results in a turnover.
  • Q4
    Catching the disc in the opponent's end zone scores a point. — True. Receiving a pass inside the opponent's end zone earns 1 point.
  • Q5
    "Frisbee" and "Flying Disc" mean the same thing. — False. "Frisbee" is a Wham-O registered trademark, so official sports use "Flying Disc."
