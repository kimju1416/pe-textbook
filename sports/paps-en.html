PAPS

Analyzing your fitness scientifically — Student Health Fitness Assessment

Introduction

What Is PAPS?

Physical Activity Promotion System — A national system that scientifically measures and evaluates student fitness to promote health

  • 1
    Official Name — Physical Activity Promotion System (PAPS). It objectively and scientifically diagnoses students' fitness levels.
  • 2
    Mandatory Nationwide — Administered at least once a year from 5th grade (elementary) through 12th grade (high school). Every school in the nation participates in this national-level fitness assessment.
  • 3
    Health Promotion Purpose — PAPS is not about ranking who is better or worse; it is a starting point for understanding your own fitness level and building health-related fitness.
2009 Launched nationwide Replaced the former "Physical Fitness Test" system Measures health-related fitness + optional fitness
Assessment System

Purpose and Grading

Your fitness is assessed across five grades, and you receive a personalized exercise prescription

  • 1
    Grade 1 (Excellent) — Outstanding health fitness. Maintain your current fitness level while challenging yourself with higher-intensity activities.
  • 2
    Grade 2 (Good) — Good health fitness. Keep exercising consistently and aim for Grade 1.
  • 3
    Grade 3 (Average) — Average fitness. Focus on strengthening weaker areas to build balanced fitness.
  • 4
    Grade 4 (Low Fitness) — Below-average fitness. Participate in basic fitness improvement programs to reduce health risks.
  • 5
    Grade 5 (Very Low Fitness) — Urgent health management needed. Participation in special programs such as the school Health Fitness Classroom is essential.

PAPS is a health management tool, not a competition. The key is to track your fitness changes each year and improve steadily!

Fitness Component 1 — Cardiorespiratory Endurance

PACER (Shuttle Run)

Measures the ability of your heart and lungs to deliver oxygen — how long you can keep running

  • 1
    What Does It Measure? — Cardiorespiratory endurance is the ability of the heart, lungs, and blood vessels to supply oxygen to muscles. It is a foundational fitness component for sustained exercise and one of the most important health indicators.
  • 2
    Test Method (Shuttle Run) — Run back and forth between two lines 20 m apart in time with a beep. Starting at Level 1 (8.5 km/h), the speed increases by 0.5 km/h every minute. You must touch the opposite line with at least one foot before the beep.
  • 3
    Termination — Failing to reach the line before the beep counts as one warning. Two consecutive failures end the test. Your total number of completed laps is your score.
  • 4
    Alternative: Step Test — Step up and down a platform (~30 cm) for 3 minutes at a tempo of 24 steps/min, then measure heart rate between 1:00 and 1:30 post-exercise. A lower heart rate indicates better cardiovascular fitness.
  • 5
    Alternative: Endurance Run/Walk — Run or walk a set distance on a track (boys 1,600 m / girls 1,200 m). A shorter completion time earns a higher grade.
Grade 1 Shuttle run: Boys 72+ / Girls 47+ Grade 3 Boys 41–56 / Girls 23–34 Grade 5 Boys ~25 / Girls ~14

Tip: Save your energy early! Jog through levels 1–4, and take short steps when turning to reduce energy expenditure.

Fitness Component 2 — Flexibility

Sit-and-Reach

Measures joint range of motion and the extensibility of muscles and ligaments

  • 1
    What Does It Measure? — Flexibility is the maximum range of motion available at a joint. It primarily evaluates the flexibility of the lower back and hamstrings, and is important for injury prevention and maintaining proper posture.
  • 2
    Test Method — (1) Remove shoes and sit with legs straight in front of the measurement box (2) Press the soles of your feet flat against the box (3) Overlap your hands and reach forward as far as possible (4) Push steadily without bouncing and hold for at least 2 seconds (5) Record the cm value where your fingertips reach.
  • 3
    Invalid Criteria — If your knees bend, the attempt is invalid! An assistant may gently press your knees down. Using bouncing momentum is also invalid.
  • 4
    Reading the Score — The baseline (0 cm) is at your toes. Reaching past your toes is recorded as a (+) value; falling short is a (-) value. Two attempts are taken and the better score is used.
Grade 1 Boys +16.1 cm+ / Girls +18.2 cm+ Grade 3 Boys +5.9–11.3 cm / Girls +10.1–15.1 cm Grade 5 Boys -2.1 cm or less / Girls +1.9 cm or less

Tip: Stretching for 5+ minutes before the test can improve your score! The most effective approach is consistent daily stretching for 10 minutes.

Fitness Component 3 — Muscular Strength · Endurance

Grip Strength & Curl-ups

Measures the maximum force a muscle can produce in one effort (strength) and the ability to exert force repeatedly (endurance)

  • 1
    What Is Muscular Strength? — The maximum force a muscle can produce in a single contraction. Needed for lifting heavy objects or pushing with force. In PAPS, it is measured with a grip strength test.
  • 2
    Grip Strength Test Method — (1) Adjust the dynamometer grip so the second finger joint is at a right angle (2) Stand upright with arms naturally at your sides, about 15 degrees from your body (3) Keep elbows straight and squeeze as hard as you can for 3 seconds (4) Two attempts per hand; the best score (kg) is used. Swinging your arm or leaning your body invalidates the attempt!
  • 3
    What Is Muscular Endurance? — The ability of muscles to repeatedly exert force over time. Essential for activities like long-distance running and climbing stairs. In PAPS, it is measured with curl-ups.
  • 4
    Curl-up Test Method — (1) Lie on a mat with knees bent at 90 degrees and feet flat on the floor (2) Cross arms over your chest in an X (3) Curl up for 1 minute on the signal (4) Your shoulder blades only need to lift off the floor to count as one rep — you do not need to sit all the way up (5) Using momentum, lifting your hips, or uncrossing your hands invalidates the rep.
  • 5
    Note: Push-ups — Some schools use push-ups as an alternative for muscular endurance. Place hands shoulder-width apart and lower your chest to about one fist height from the floor, then push back up. Girls may perform on their knees.
Grip Str. Grade 1 Boys 37.1 kg+ / Girls 24.1 kg+ Grip Str. Grade 3 Boys 24.8–31.4 kg / Girls 15.5–19.8 kg Curl-ups Grade 1 Boys 51+ / Girls 33+ Curl-ups Grade 3 Boys 26–39 / Girls 16–24

Tip: Warm your hands before the grip test! For curl-ups, focus on a steady rhythm rather than speed.

Fitness Component 4 — Power

50 m Dash & Standing Long Jump

Measures the ability to generate explosive force in a short time

  • 1
    What Is Power? — The ability to produce large amounts of force quickly (Power = Force x Speed). Important in nearly every sport, including jumping, throwing, and sprinting.
  • 2
    50 m Dash Method — (1) Take a standing or crouch start position behind the start line (2) Sprint at full speed on the command "On your mark — Set — Go!" (3) Measured to 0.01 seconds with an electronic timer (4) Do not slow down — sprint through the finish line.
  • 3
    Standing Long Jump Method — (1) Stand with both feet together behind the takeoff line (2) Swing your arms back for momentum (3) Bend your knees deeply, then jump forward with both feet simultaneously (4) If you fall backward, the point where your hips touch is your score (5) Measured in cm from the takeoff line to the nearest landing point. Two attempts; better score is used.
  • 4
    Cautions — 50 m Dash: Two false starts result in disqualification. Crossing into another lane invalidates the attempt. Standing Long Jump: Stepping on the line or jumping off one foot invalidates the attempt.
50 m Grade 1 Boys under 7.5 s / Girls under 8.8 s 50 m Grade 3 Boys 8.2–8.8 s / Girls 9.8–10.5 s Long Jump Grade 1 Boys 230 cm+ / Girls 181 cm+

Tip: For the 50 m, reaction time at the start is key! For the standing long jump, swing your arms wide and aim for a 45-degree launch angle.

Fitness Component 5 — Body Composition

BMI & Body Fat Percentage

Examining the ratio of fat and muscle that make up our bodies

  • 1
    What Is Body Composition? — Body composition refers to the proportions of fat, muscle, bone, and water in the body. Too much body fat increases the risk of obesity-related diseases (diabetes, cardiovascular disease); too little can cause hormonal imbalances and weakened immunity.
  • 2
    BMI Measurement — (1) Remove shoes and measure height (m) with a stadiometer (2) Weigh yourself in light clothing on a scale (kg) (3) BMI = weight (kg) / height (m)². Example: 165 cm tall, 55 kg → 55 / 1.65² = 20.2
  • 3
    BMI Categories (Middle School Reference) — Underweight (below 18.5) / Normal (18.5–22.9) / Overweight (23.0–24.9) / Obese (25.0+). Note: For growing adolescents, age- and sex-specific percentile standards are applied, which may differ from adult criteria.
  • 4
    Body Fat % Measurement — A caliper measures skinfold thickness at sites such as the triceps and below the shoulder blade. Alternatively, a BIA (bioelectrical impedance analysis) body composition analyzer can measure automatically. Normal body fat % for middle schoolers: Boys 10–20%, Girls 17–28%.
  • 5
    Limitations of BMI — Since BMI only uses height and weight, muscular students may be classified as "overweight." Body fat percentage is a more accurate indicator, so measuring both when possible is recommended.
BMI Grade 1 Normal range (both sexes) Grade 4–5 Severe obesity or underweight Body fat % is more precise than BMI

Tip: Don't obsess over BMI numbers! Consider muscle mass and growth stage as well. Consistent exercise and a balanced diet matter most.

Exercise Prescription

Exercise Prescription by Grade

Based on your PAPS results, let's create an exercise plan tailored to you

🥇 Grades 1–2 (Maintain + Advance)

Your fitness is already excellent! Maintain your current exercise habits while challenging yourself with higher-intensity activities. Stay motivated through sports clubs and record-breaking goals.

🥈 Grade 3 (Balance & Strengthen)

Average level, but don't get complacent! Focus on strengthening weaker fitness components to achieve balanced fitness. Train your weak areas at least 3 times per week.

🏃 Grades 4–5 (Basic Fitness Program)

Join your school's "Health Fitness Classroom" program. Start with walking and jogging, gradually increasing intensity. Don't overdo it — be consistent! Small achievements lead to big changes.

📅 Exercise 3+ Times Per Week

The WHO recommends at least 60 minutes of moderate-to-vigorous physical activity daily for adolescents. Aim for at least 3 sessions per week intense enough to break a sweat.

Your PAPS report includes individual grade results for each item along with a personalized exercise prescription. Be sure to review it and put it into practice!

Safety Rules

Safety & Precautions

Essential guidelines for accurate and safe testing

🩺 Check Your Health

Always check your health status before testing. Students with heart conditions, asthma, recent surgery, etc. must inform the teacher in advance. Do not push yourself on days when you feel unwell.

🚫 No Overexertion

If you experience dizziness, chest pain, or difficulty breathing during a test, stop immediately and tell the teacher. Pushing too hard for a better grade is actually dangerous.

📐 Proper Test Form

Accurate results require proper form. Watch the teacher's demonstration carefully before each test, and avoid fouls such as bending your knees or using momentum.

🤸 Warm Up Before Testing

Always warm up for at least 10 minutes. Loosen your joints and muscles with joint circles, light jogging, and stretching. This is the first step in injury prevention!

Quiz

True / False Quiz

Let's check what you have learned!

  • Q1
    PAPS is administered starting from 1st grade of elementary school. — False. It is administered from 5th grade (elementary) through 12th grade (high school), at least once per year.
  • Q2
    In the PACER shuttle run, the test ends after two consecutive failures to reach the line. — True. Failing to reach the opposite line before the beep counts as one miss, and two consecutive misses end the test.
  • Q3
    In curl-ups, you must sit all the way up for a rep to count. — False. Your shoulder blades only need to lift off the floor; you do not need to sit all the way up.
  • Q4
    BMI is calculated by dividing weight (kg) by height (m) squared. — True. Example: 50 kg, 160 cm → 50 / 1.6² = 19.5.
  • Q5
    PAPS Grade 5 means excellent fitness. — False. Grade 5 indicates very low fitness requiring urgent health management; Grade 1 is the best.
