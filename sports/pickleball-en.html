Pickleball

A hybrid sport combining tennis, badminton, and table tennis — discover the world's fastest-growing sport that anyone can enjoy within 30 minutes.

Introduction

What Is Pickleball?

A racket sport played on a badminton-sized court with solid paddles and a perforated plastic ball

  • 1
    Hybrid — A unique sport combining the strategy of tennis, the court of badminton, and the paddle of table tennis
  • 2
    Low barrier to entry — Simple rules and easy to learn; you can start playing within 30 minutes
  • 3
    All-ages sport — Enjoyable for everyone from children to seniors, regardless of fitness level
Global craze Fastest-growing sport in the USA Doubles popular 2-player team games Playable indoors and outdoors
History · Origins

A Fun History

A story that began in the summer of 1965 on Bainbridge Island, Washington, USA

  • 1
    Summer 1965 — Joel Pritchard and Bill Bell improvised a new game when their children were bored on a weekend
  • 2
    Birth of the equipment — Unable to find a shuttlecock, they substituted table tennis paddles and a perforated plastic ball
  • 3
    The third founder — Neighbor Barney McCallum joined and helped formalize and organize the official rules
  • 4
    Origin of the name — Two theories exist: (1) Named after the "Pickle Boat" in rowing, which collects leftover rowers; (2) Named after the family dog "Pickles" (though the dog was actually adopted after the game was invented)

The three founders — Joel Pritchard (congressman), Bill Bell (businessman), and Barney McCallum (businessman). What started as a backyard game became a worldwide sport!

Rules · Court

Court and Equipment

The structure of a pickleball court and the equipment needed

  • 🏓
    Paddle — A racket with a solid face. Larger than a table tennis paddle but smaller than a tennis racket. Available in wood, composite, graphite, and more.
  • ⚪
    Ball — A lightweight perforated plastic ball (similar to a wiffle ball). Indoor balls have 26 holes (softer), outdoor balls have 40 holes (harder, wind-resistant).
  • 👟
    Attire — Court shoes (non-slip), comfortable athletic wear. Tennis shoes recommended.
Rules · Serve

Serve Rules

Learn pickleball's unique serving method

  • 1
    Underhand only — The serve must be made with an upward swing from below the waist. Overhand serves are prohibited!
  • 2
    Diagonal serve — Served from your service area diagonally to the opponent's service area
  • 3
    Behind the baseline — Both feet must be behind the baseline when serving
  • 4
    Only serving team scores — Only the team serving can earn points (side-out scoring)
  • 5
    Serve order — In doubles, when the first server loses the rally, it passes to the partner; when both lose, serve passes to the opposing team

Drop serve — Since 2021, a "drop serve" where you drop the ball and hit it after it bounces has been officially permitted.

Rules · Core

Two-Bounce Rule

The most crucial rule that defines pickleball!

  • 1
    1st bounce (receive) — After the serve lands on the opponent's court, the receiving team must let it bounce once before returning. Volleying the serve is a fault!
  • 2
    2nd bounce (serving team's return) — The receiving team's return must also bounce once on the serving team's court before being hit. Volleying the return is a fault!
  • 3
    Free play afterwards — After both bounces have occurred, volleys (hitting without a bounce) are freely allowed

Why does this rule exist? — It prevents serve-and-volley rush strategies, making rallies longer and the game more fun and fair. This rule creates pickleball's unique strategic depth!

Rules · Kitchen

Non-Volley Zone (Kitchen)

Nicknamed the "Kitchen" — the most important zone in pickleball

  • 📏
    Location and size — A rectangular zone extending 7 feet (2.1m) from each side of the net
  • 🚫
    Core rule — You cannot volley (hit the ball before it bounces) while standing in this zone
  • 👟
    Line counts too — Stepping on the kitchen line is also a fault! Even if your toe barely touches it, you lose the point
  • 🏃
    Momentum counts — If your momentum carries you into the kitchen after a volley, it is still a fault. You must stop and maintain your balance
  • ✅
    Exception — You may enter the kitchen and hit the ball if the ball bounces inside the kitchen first (responding to dinks)

Origin of the name "Kitchen" — It is widely believed to come from the shuffleboard game's penalty zone called the "kitchen." It means a zone where entering puts you at a disadvantage!

Rules · Scoring

Scoring and Game Format

Pickleball's unique score-calling system

📢 Doubles Score Call

"(Serving team score)-(Receiving team score)-(Server number)"
Example: "3-2-1" = Serving team 3 pts, Receiving team 2 pts, 1st server

🏆 Winning Condition

First to 11 points (must win by 2 or more). Tournaments may use 15 or 21 points.

🔄 Side-Out

In doubles: 1st server loses rally → 2nd server takes over → 2nd server loses rally → Serve passes to opponents!

⚡ Game Start Exception

At game start, the first serving team has only one server (starts at 0-0-2). A rule for balance!

Key Skills 1

Serve and Return

Two fundamentals that determine how the game starts

  • 1
    Underhand serve — Pull the arm back and swing naturally upward from below the waist. Do not over-bend the wrist.
  • 2
    Deep serve — Place the serve deep near the opponent's baseline to push them back. A short serve gives them an attacking opportunity.
  • 3
    Deep return — Remember the two-bounce rule and return deep! The key is preventing the serving team from advancing to the net.
Key Skills 2

Dink and Drop Shot

The art of pickleball — mastering soft touch

  • 1
    Dink — A soft shot from near the kitchen that barely clears the net. A key control technique that prevents the opponent from hitting hard.
  • 2
    Drop shot — A soft shot from behind the baseline that drops in front of the kitchen. It disrupts the defensive flow and buys time to advance to the net.
  • 3
    Third shot drop — Using a drop shot for the serving team's 3rd shot (serve → return → 3rd shot). A key strategy for gaining net control after the two-bounce rule!
Key Skills 3

Smash and Rally

Balancing offense and defense

  • 1
    Smash — A powerful overhead attack hitting a high ball downward. Most effective when attempted outside the kitchen.
  • 2
    Lob — A defensive shot sent high over the opponent's head at the net. Pushes the opponent back and disrupts their positioning.
  • 3
    Rally strategy — In pickleball, patience beats power! Reducing your errors and forcing your opponent to make mistakes is the key to victory.
Tactics · Strategy

Basic Tactics

Essential doubles strategies that will boost your game

🎯 Control the Kitchen

Securing position at the kitchen line is the heart of the game. The team standing at the kitchen takes control.

🏓 Dink Wars

A patience battle exchanging dinks at the kitchen. The first to attempt a hard shot faces a higher risk of losing the point.

📏 Deep Serve/Return

Send both serves and returns deep to the opponent's baseline to push them back. Short shots give them opportunities.

👥 Side by Side

The basic doubles formation is standing side by side. Each player covers half the court to minimize open space.

🎯 Target Weaknesses

Focus attacks on the opponent's backhand. The middle gap between two players is also an effective target!

Safety Guidelines

Playing Safely

Precautions for enjoying pickleball without injury

👁️ Eye Protection

Pickleball balls fly fast! Be especially careful when receiving smashes at the net. Wearing protective eyewear is recommended.

🩹 Slip Caution

Indoor courts can be slippery. Be careful during sudden direction changes and wear non-slip court shoes.

💪 Shoulder/Elbow Protection

Repetitive swinging can lead to shoulder and elbow injuries (tennis elbow). Adequate stretching and rest are essential.

✅ Preparation Checklist

Indoor shoes (non-marking), adequate hydration, at least 5 minutes of warm-up, communication with partner (ball calls: "Mine!" "Yours!").

Quiz

True/False Quiz

Let's check what you've learned!

  • Q1
    Pickleball serves can be made overhand. — False. Pickleball serves must be made underhand, from below the waist.
  • Q2
    You can never hit the ball in the Non-Volley Zone (Kitchen) under any circumstances. — False. You can enter and hit the ball if it bounces inside the kitchen first.
  • Q3
    The two-bounce rule means both the serve and return must bounce once before being hit. — True. Both the serve and the return must bounce once before being struck.
  • Q4
    Pickleball doubles score calls consist of two numbers. — False. In doubles, the call consists of three numbers: "serving team score - receiving team score - server number."
  • Q5
    In pickleball, only the serving team can score points. — True. Under the side-out scoring system, only the team with the serve can score.
