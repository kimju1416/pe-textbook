Badminton

Introduction

What is Badminton?

A racket sport where players hit a shuttlecock back and forth across a net

  • 1
    Net Competition Sport — Hit the shuttlecock over the net with a racket to the opponent's court. If it touches the floor, the opponent loses the point.
  • 2
    Singles · Doubles · Mixed Doubles — 1v1 singles, 2v2 doubles, and mixed doubles. The Olympics features all 5 events (men's/women's singles and doubles + mixed doubles).
  • 3
    Shuttlecock top speed: 493 km/h — The world-record shuttlecock speed! Far exceeding tennis (263 km/h) or baseball (169 km/h) — the fastest projectile in racket sports.
  • 4
    Korea is a badminton powerhouse — With numerous Olympic gold medals, Korea is a traditional badminton powerhouse with consistently impressive performances on the world stage.

History

History of Badminton

From a traditional Indian game to an Olympic sport

  • 1
    Indian Poona Game — Badminton's roots trace to the traditional Indian game 'Poona' — hitting a feathered ball by hand or paddle.
  • 2
    1873: Badminton House, England — British soldiers who served in India brought the game back. Formal rules were established at 'Badminton House' in Gloucestershire, which gave the sport its name.
  • 3
    BWF (Badminton World Federation) — Founded in 1934 as the IBF, later renamed BWF. Currently 190+ member nations.
  • 4
    1992 Barcelona Olympics — Badminton became an official Olympic sport. Korea's Park Joo-bong and Kim Moon-soo won men's doubles gold.
  • 5
    Korea's golden era — Stars like Park Joo-bong, Bang Soo-hyun, Lee Yong-dae, and Lee Hyo-jung dominated the world stage. Korea is a perennial Olympic medal contender.
  • 6
    Modern star — An Se-young — 2024 Paris Olympics women's singles gold medalist. Leading a new golden era for Korean badminton.
Court · Facilities

Badminton Court

Court width changes depending on singles vs doubles

  • 📐
    Court Size — 13.4m long, 6.1m wide (doubles) / 5.18m (singles). Doubles uses the outer sidelines, singles uses the inner.
  • 🥅
    Net Height — 1.524m at center, 1.55m at the posts. The top of the net is slightly higher at the edges.
  • 📏
    Short Service Line — 1.98m from the net. Serves must clear this line to be valid.
  • 🟡
    Doubles Long Service Line — In doubles, serves must land inside this line. In singles, the back line is used.
Rules · Match

Match Rules

Win 2 out of 3 games to win the match!

🏆 Best of 3 Games

Win 2 out of 3 games. Each game is won by the first player/team to reach 21 points.

🏸 Rally Point System

Every rally earns a point regardless of who served. Win the rally = 1 point!

⚖️ Deuce Rule

At 20-20, play continues until there's a 2-point lead. At 29-29, the first to 30 wins!

↗️ Serve Direction

Serves must go diagonally! Even score = serve from right; odd score = serve from left.

⏸️ Interval

When one side reaches 11 points, a 60-second break. Between games: 120 seconds (2 min) rest.

🔄 Court Change

Switch courts after game 1. In game 3, switch when one side reaches 11 points.

Rules · Serve

Serve Rules

Badminton serves have strict rules

  • 1
    Hit Below Waist (115cm) — At the moment of contact, the entire shuttlecock must be below the server's waist (115cm from the floor). Overhand serves are a fault!
  • 2
    Feet Must Stay on Floor — Part of both feet must be touching the court floor at the moment of serve. Jumping or dragging feet = fault.
  • 3
    Diagonal to Service Court — Serves must go diagonally to the opponent's service court. Must land between the short and long service lines.
  • 4
    Singles Serve Order — The serving player continues to serve. Win a point = switch service courts and serve again; lose = serve passes to opponent.
  • 5
    Doubles Serve Order — When the serving team loses, serve passes to the opponent. Only at the start does one player serve before it switches; after that, both partners alternate.
Remember: The serve is the only play in badminton that starts from a standstill. You can employ diverse tactics while following the rules!
Core Skill 1

Serve

The start of the game — learn serve techniques

  • 1
    Short Serve — Send the shuttlecock just over the net near the short service line. Makes it difficult for the opponent to attack aggressively.
  • 2
    Long Serve — Hit the shuttlecock high and deep to the opponent's back line. Used mainly in singles to push the opponent back.
  • 3
    Flick Serve — Fake a short serve, then quickly flick it deep with a wrist snap. A deceptive advanced technique.
  • 4
    Backhand Short Serve — The standard doubles serve. Hold the racket in front with a backhand grip and gently send the shuttlecock just over the net.
Core Skill 2

Smash

The crown of overhead strokes — a powerful attacking shot

  • 1
    Overhead Stroke — The basic stance for hitting shuttlecocks high above. Clears, smashes, and drops all start from this position.
  • 2
    Clear — Send the shuttlecock high and deep to the back of the opponent's court. A defensive stroke that buys time to regroup.
  • 3
    Smash — Strike the shuttlecock at the highest point, driving it down steeply. The most powerful attack — called the "flower of badminton."
  • 4
    Drop — From a smash stance, gently let the shuttlecock fall just over the net. Catches the opponent off guard.
  • 5
    Footwork — Move backward, jump, and strike while airborne. Quick backward movement and mid-air balance are key.
Core Skill 3

Rally Techniques

Dominate the court with diverse rally skills and footwork

  • 1
    Net Shot (Hairpin) — Gently lift the shuttlecock right at the net to send it tumbling just over. Forces the opponent to hit upward.
  • 2
    Drive — A fast, flat strike at net height. Frequently used in doubles for quick offensive exchanges.
  • 3
    Push — Quickly push the shuttlecock downward from above the net. Quickly dispatches high returns from opponents.
  • 4
    Lift (Lob) — Send the shuttlecock high and deep from a low position near the net. Buys time to recover position.
  • 5
    Footwork Pattern (6-Direction Movement) — From center court, move quickly to 6 directions: front-left/right, side-left/right, back-left/right and return. The foundation of all techniques!
Tactics · Strategy

Badminton Tactics

Skill alone isn't enough — use your brain to win!

📐 4-Corner Attack

Alternate shots to the 4 corners (front-left, front-right, back-left, back-right) to make the opponent run diagonally. Drains stamina and creates open space.

⚡ Speed Variation

Alternate smash (fast) → drop (slow) → clear (high) to vary shuttlecock speed. Disrupts the opponent's rhythm and timing!

🎯 Center Position

Always return to center court after each shot. From center you can reach any direction fastest, maximizing defensive coverage.

👥 Doubles — Front-Back Formation

Attack in front-back (front-rear), defend side-by-side. Switch formations as needed. Partner coordination is key to doubles victory.

🔙 Target the Backhand

Most players have a weaker backhand. Concentrating attacks there increases the chance of weak returns.

🧠 Read the Game

Observe the opponent's stance, racket direction, and court position to predict the next shot. The player who reads one step ahead dominates the rally.

Safety Guidelines

Playing Safely

Essential safety tips for injury prevention and long-term enjoyment

💪 Shoulder Injury Risk

Repetitive overhead swings strain the rotator cuff. Prevent with thorough shoulder stretching and strength exercises.

🦵 Knee Injury Risk

Frequent lunges and sharp direction changes stress knee ligaments. Learn proper lunge form.

🦶 Ankle Injury Risk

Sharp turns and jump landings can twist ankles. Wear badminton shoes with good ankle support.

👁️ Eye Injury Risk

A shuttlecock to the eye can cause serious injury. In doubles, the front player should watch out for partner's smashes!

🦿 Achilles Tendon Risk

Repetitive heel loading from footwork stresses the Achilles. Stretch calves thoroughly before play!

🤸 Stretching Required!

Warm up shoulders, wrists, knees, ankles, calves in order. Cool down after the game to relieve muscle fatigue.

Fun Facts

Badminton Trivia

How much do you know about badminton?

  • Q1
    How many feathers on a shuttlecock? — Answer: 16! Made from 16 goose feathers, weighing about 4.74–5.50g.
  • Q2
    Which country has the most Olympic badminton golds? — Answer: China! The all-time leader. Korea and Indonesia follow.
  • Q3
    Longest rally in a badminton match? — In 2017, a rally of 108 consecutive hits was recorded. About 2+ minutes without the shuttlecock touching the floor!
  • Q4
    Why does a shuttlecock slow down so quickly? — Air resistance from the feathers! It leaves the racket at 300+ km/h but reaches the opponent at only ~60–70 km/h.
Fun fact: Pro players go through 10–12 shuttlecocks per match. Bent feathers alter the flight trajectory!

Quiz

True/False Quiz

Let's check what you've learned!

  • Q1
    The world record shuttlecock speed is about 493 km/h. — True. The fastest projectile speed in racket sports.
  • Q2
    A badminton match is best of 3 sets, first to win 2. — True. Both singles and doubles use best of 3 games format.
  • Q3
    You can serve the shuttlecock from above your waist. — False. The shuttlecock must be below the waist (115cm) at the moment of contact.
  • Q4
    In doubles, serves must go diagonally. — True. Both singles and doubles require diagonal serves to the service court.
  • Q5
    A drop shot sends the shuttlecock high and deep. — False. A drop shot gently places it just over the net. A clear sends it high and deep.
