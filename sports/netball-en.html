Netball

Basketball's cousin — a strategic team sport where you move the ball only by passing

Introduction

What Is Netball?

A 7-a-side team sport where you score by shooting into a ring with no backboard, moving the ball only through passing — no dribbling allowed

  • 1
    No dribbling, only passing — Unlike basketball, you cannot bounce the ball. You must advance it through passes only, making teamwork the true measure of skill
  • 2
    Position-restricted zones — Each of the 7 players can only play in designated zones. You cannot freely roam the entire court, making role division essential
  • 3
    Non-contact principle — Physical contact with opponents is prohibited. A minimum distance of 0.9m (3 feet) must be maintained, ensuring safe and fair play
In short — A sport that takes basketball, removes dribbling, and maximizes strategy and passing!
History · Development

History of Netball

A team sport born from basketball that spread worldwide

  • 1
    Derived from basketball in 1891 — Shortly after James Naismith invented basketball, it began as a modified sport for women. Initially called "women's basketball"
  • 2
    Developed in Britain and Commonwealth nations — Countries like Britain, Australia, New Zealand, and Jamaica refined independent rules, growing it into an official sport
  • 3
    80+ countries, 20 million participants worldwide — Currently over 80 countries are members of the International Netball Federation (INF), with approximately 20 million players globally
  • 4
    Netball World Cup — The World Cup is held every 4 years, with Australia and New Zealand as powerhouses. Olympic inclusion is also being pursued
  • 5
    Ideal for school PE — Thanks to non-contact rules and position-based roles, it is gaining attention as a safe sport where all students can participate
Rules · Equipment

Court and Equipment

Learn the structure of the netball court and key equipment

  • 1
    Court size — 30.5m x 15.25m, divided into 3 thirds: center third and 2 goal thirds
  • 2
    Goal circle — A semicircle with a 4.9m radius around the goal post. Shooting is only allowed from inside!
  • 3
    Goal post — Height 3.05m, with no backboard unlike basketball — precise shooting is essential
  • 4
    Ball — Size 5 ball (circumference 69-71cm, weight 400-450g)
Rules

Game Rules

Learn the core rules of netball

⚡ Game Operations

- Official game: 4 quarters x 15 min (school: 8 min)
- Each quarter starts with a center pass
- Only Goal Shooter (GS) and Goal Attack (GA) can shoot
- Shooting is only allowed from inside the goal circle

🚫 Foul Situations

- No dribbling — Once you catch the ball, only pivot foot movement is allowed
- 3-second rule — Must pass or shoot within 3 seconds of catching
- Zone violation — Leaving your designated zone is a foul
- Contact foul — Getting within 0.9m of an opponent is a foul

Positions

7 Positions

Netball's most unique feature! Each position has designated zones they can play in

  • GS
    Goal Shooter — Restricted to the attacking goal circle only. The key scorer responsible for shooting inside the goal circle
  • GA
    Goal Attack — Center third + attacking goal third + goal circle. An offensive all-rounder who can also shoot
  • WA
    Wing Attack — Center third + attacking goal third (excluding goal circle). The key pass connector for offensive plays
  • C
    Centre — Center third + both goal thirds (excluding goal circles). The widest range of movement, linking offense and defense
  • WD
    Wing Defence — Center third + defensive goal third (excluding goal circle). Marks the opposing wing attack and intercepts passes
  • GD
    Goal Defence — Center third + defensive goal third + goal circle. The defensive core who stops the opposing goal attack
  • GK
    Goal Keeper — Restricted to the defensive goal circle only. Closely marks the opposing goal shooter and disrupts shots
Skills 1

Shooting

  • 1
    One-hand shot (basic) — Bend knees → extend arm → wrist snap in sequence. The key is naturally transferring power from bottom to top
  • 2
    Two-hand shot — Suitable for beginners. Push the ball upward from chest level with both hands
  • 3
    Stepping shot — Step forward while shooting. Closes the distance to the defender and increases accuracy
  • 4
    Fake then shoot — Pretend to pass → defender reacts → shoot into open space. The essence of mind games!
Tip — With no backboard, you must put it right through the ring. An arching trajectory is crucial!
Skills 2

Passing

  • 1
    Chest pass — Push out from chest height with both hands. Accurate and fast for short distances
  • 2
    Shoulder pass — A long pass thrown with one hand from above the shoulder. Used to reach distant teammates
  • 3
    Bounce pass — Bounce once off the floor. Effective for threading through tight defensive spaces
  • 4
    Lob pass — A high arc pass thrown over the defense. Advantageous for taller teammates
  • 5
    Receiving — The skill of catching while landing stably. The key is landing on one foot and establishing a pivot foot
Skills 3

Defense

  • 1
    0.9m distance (3-foot rule) — Must stay at least 0.9m (3 feet) from the ball carrier. Extend arms to disrupt passes from this distance
  • 2
    Intercept — Read the opponent's passing lanes and snatch the ball mid-air. The highlight of defense!
  • 3
    Rebound — Quickly secure the ball under the ring when an opponent's shot misses
  • 4
    Body positioning — Position yourself between the opponent and the ball to block passing lanes. Strategic defense that dominates space without contact
Tactics · Strategy

Tactics

Team tactics for winning at netball

⚔️ Offensive Tactics

- Center pass strategy — Execute pre-planned patterns quickly at the start of each quarter
- Create 2:1 in goal circle — GS and GA shake off 1 defender for a free shooting opportunity
- Screen play — Teammates create a wall to make space for the shooter
- Dodge/Lead run — Suddenly change direction to lose the defender and sprint to open space for a pass

🛡️ Defensive Tactics

- Quick defensive transition — Immediately return to defensive positions after a failed attack
- 1:1 tight marking — Each position persistently follows their corresponding opponent
- Pass lane blocking — Target intercepts while reducing opponent's passing options
- Space pressure — Within the 3-foot rule, maximize restriction of the opponent's vision and passing angles

Safety

Safety Guidelines

Precautions for safe and enjoyable netball

⚠️ Cautions

- Landing injuries — Knees and ankles can be strained after jump shots or rebounds. Practice stable two-foot landings
- Finger injuries — Fingers can bend back when receiving fast passes. Develop the habit of catching softly with both hands
- Follow the 3-foot rule — Not maintaining distance can lead to collision injuries. Always maintain at least 0.9m

🏃 Warm-up

- Ankle/knee stretching — Many direction changes and jump landings, so warm up lower joints thoroughly
- Shoulder/wrist rotation — Shooting and passing heavily use shoulders and wrists, so warm-up is essential
- Light pass practice — Exchange various types of passes with teammates before the game
- Footwork practice — Lightly practice pivots, direction changes, and basic footwork

Quiz

True/False Quiz

Let's check what you've learned!

  • Q1
    In netball, you can dribble the ball to move it like in basketball. — False. Dribbling is prohibited in netball; you must advance the ball through passes only.
  • Q2
    In netball, all players can shoot. — False. Only the Goal Shooter (GS) and Goal Attack (GA) can shoot from inside the goal circle.
  • Q3
    In netball, you must pass or shoot within 3 seconds of catching the ball. — True. Violating the 3-second rule results in a foul.
  • Q4
    Netball goal posts have a backboard like basketball. — False. Netball goal posts have no backboard, so precise shooting is essential.
  • Q5
    In netball, defenders must maintain at least 0.9m (3 feet) distance from an opponent. — True. This is the 3-foot rule; defending without contact is the principle.
