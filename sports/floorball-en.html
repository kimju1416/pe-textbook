Floorball

The thrill of hockey brought indoors — a stick sport for everyone

Introduction

What Is Floorball?

A hockey-style sport played indoors with plastic sticks and a perforated ball

  • 1
    Indoor version of ice hockey — Hockey on a gym floor instead of ice. All you need is a stick and a ball — no complicated gear required!
  • 2
    Safe play — Lightweight plastic equipment keeps injury risk low, with minimal protective gear needed
  • 3
    Fast-paced action — Dynamic gameplay with rapid transitions between offense and defense on a compact court
Origin Sweden Feature Simplified equipment Popular school sport
History · Development

History of Floorball

A sport that started in Swedish PE classes and spread worldwide

  • 1
    1970s Sweden — Developed for school PE classes as a simple indoor alternative to ice hockey
  • 2
    1986 IFF founded — The International Floorball Federation was established, elevating it to an international sport
  • 3
    Spread across Europe and Asia — Rapidly expanded to Finland, Switzerland, Czech Republic, and Asian countries like Japan and Singapore
  • 4
    Introduction to Korea — Became a popular school sports club activity with national tournaments
  • 5
    World Championships — As of 2024, the number of host countries continues to grow, and the sport is being promoted for Olympic inclusion
Rules · Equipment

Court and Equipment

Learn the structure of a floorball court and the required equipment

  • 🏒
    Stick — Carbon/fiberglass material, max length 114cm. The blade (tip) is plastic — lightweight and safe.
  • ⚪
    Ball — A plastic ball with 26 holes, diameter 72mm. Lightweight and rolls smoothly on the floor.
  • 🧤
    Goalkeeper gear — Helmet, upper and lower body pads. The goalkeeper blocks with their body only — no stick!
Rules · Game

Game Rules

Basic rules of a floorball game

👥 Team Composition

5 field players + 1 goalkeeper = 6 per team. Adjustable for school classes based on available players.

⏱️ Game Duration

3 periods x 20 minutes (60 min total). For school classes: 2 periods x 10 min, etc. Includes breaks between periods.

🏒 Kick-off

The game starts and restarts after goals with a kick-off at center court. Opponents must be at least 3m away.

🤚 No Hand Contact

Only the goalkeeper can use hands inside the goal area. Field players touching the ball with their hands is a foul!

📏 High-sticking

Raising the stick above knee height to hit the ball is a foul. The most important safety rule!

🔄 Rolling Substitution

Players can be substituted freely without stopping play. Quick switches in the substitution zone!

Rules · Fouls

Major Fouls

Fouls you must know for a safe and fair game

📐 High-sticking

Swinging the stick above knee height. The most common and dangerous foul — absolutely prohibited!

⚔️ Slashing

Hitting or striking the opponent's stick hard. Deliberate strikes not aimed at the ball are fouls.

🦵 Tripping

Using your stick or foot to trip an opponent. Can cause serious injuries and is strictly penalized.

✋ Handball

A field player touching the ball with their hand or arm. The goalkeeper also cannot use hands outside the goal area.

🎯 Free Hit

When a foul occurs, a free hit is taken from the spot of the foul. Opponents must be at least 3m away.

⚡ Penalty Shot

A penalty shot is awarded for fouls inside the goal area. A 1-on-1 shooting opportunity against the goalkeeper from center!

Key Skills 1

Stick Handling

The fundamentals of ball control

  • 1
    Basic grip — Hands about 30cm apart. Top hand (left) grips the end of the stick for steering; bottom hand (right) grips the middle for power.
  • 2
    Dribbling — Alternate touches with the front and back of the blade while moving. Keep your eyes on the field, not the ball!
  • 3
    Fake moves — Swing the stick left and right to deceive opponents. Combine with body weight shifts for effectiveness.
  • 4
    Ball shielding — Position your body between the opponent and the ball to prevent it from being stolen. Use your shoulder to create space.
Key Skills 2

Passing

The heart of team play — accurate passing techniques

  • 1
    Sweep pass — A basic pass sweeping the blade along the floor. Highly accurate and stable.
  • 2
    Slap pass — A pass lifting the stick slightly then striking down hard. Effective for long-distance delivery.
  • 3
    Backhand pass — Using the back of the blade. Hard to predict and can deceive opponents.
  • 4
    One-touch pass — Passing immediately upon receiving. Essential for quick offensive transitions!
  • 5
    Wall pass (using boards) — Bouncing the ball off the rink boards to a teammate. A creative way to bypass defenders.
Key Skills 3

Shooting

Various shooting techniques for scoring goals

  • 1
    Wrist shot — A quick shot using wrist snap. The small motion makes it difficult for the goalkeeper to react.
  • 2
    Slap shot — A power shot with a big backswing. Effective from long range.
  • 3
    Drag shot — Pulling the ball sideways while shooting to throw off the goalkeeper's timing.
  • 4
    One-timer — Shooting immediately upon receiving a pass without stopping the ball. Gives the goalkeeper no time to prepare!
  • 5
    Finding open spots — Reading the goalkeeper's positioning and targeting open spaces (five-hole, top corners, etc.). Good judgment is key.
Tactics · Positions

Positions and Tactics

Understand each position's role and basic tactics

⚡ Forwards (2)

The front line of attack. Primary job is scoring goals. Requires fast movement and accurate shooting.

🛡️ Defenders (2)

The core of defense. Block opponents' attacks and protect the goalkeeper. Start counterattacks with accurate passes.

🔗 Center (1)

The link between offense and defense. A midfielder who wins the ball and transitions to attack.

🧤 Goalkeeper

Uses hands, feet, and entire body to block goals. Plays without a stick and can only operate within the goal area.

🔺 Triangle Passing

A basic offensive tactic where 3 players pass in a triangular formation to disrupt the defense.

⚡ Counter-attack & Power Play

Fast offensive transition immediately after winning the ball. Use numerical advantage during opponent ejections for power plays.

Safety Guidelines

Playing Safely

Essential safety rules for enjoying floorball without injury

📐 Absolutely No High-sticking

Raising the stick above knee height can cause serious injuries. Always keep the stick low!

👓 Protective Eyewear Recommended

The ball can fly quickly, so sports goggles are recommended for eye protection.

🧤 Goalkeeper Gear Required

Goalkeepers must always wear a helmet and upper/lower body pads. Never play goalkeeper without protective gear!

🚧 Watch for Board Collisions

You may crash into the rink boards while focused on the game. Slow down near the boards.

🤸 Warm-up Is Essential

Especially stretch your wrists, shoulders, and lower back! Joint injury prevention is crucial due to frequent sudden direction changes.

Quiz

True/False Quiz

Let's check what you've learned!

  • Q1
    In floorball, swinging the stick above knee height is allowed. — False. High-sticking is the most important foul and is absolutely prohibited for safety.
  • Q2
    The floorball goalkeeper blocks with their body only, without a stick. — True. The goalkeeper uses hands, feet, and their entire body to block shots without a stick.
  • Q3
    In floorball, players can be freely substituted without stopping the game. — True. Rolling substitution allows players to be swapped freely during play from the substitution zone.
  • Q4
    In floorball, field players can catch and throw the ball with their hands. — False. A field player touching the ball with their hand or arm is a handball foul.
  • Q5
    Floorball was originally created in the United States. — False. It was developed in Sweden in the 1970s for school PE classes.
