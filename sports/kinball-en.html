Kin-Ball

Three teams, one giant ball — the ultimate in cooperation

Introduction

What Is Kin-Ball?

A sport using a giant ball (1.22m diameter) played by 3 teams simultaneously

  • 1
    3 teams at once — one of a kind! — While most sports pit 2 teams against each other, Kin-Ball is the world's only team sport where three teams compete simultaneously on one court
  • 2
    Giant ball — Uses an oversized ball with a diameter of 1.22m (122cm), weighing about 1kg. Too big for one person to hold, but light enough for anyone to hit
  • 3
    Full team participation required — During a hit (serve), all 4 team members must touch the ball. A true team sport that is impossible to play alone
Born in Canada — A sport created in Canada with the goal of "PE classes where no student is left out"
History · Development

History of Kin-Ball

A teacher's challenge to create PE where every student participates

  • 1
    Born in Canada, 1986 — Invented by Quebec PE teacher Mario Demers. Born from the philosophy "Let no student stand idle in the corner during class"
  • 2
    Meaning of KIN — KIN comes from "Kinesthesia" (movement sense). It embodies the idea of growing together through body movement and sensation
  • 3
    IKBF established — The International Kin-Ball Federation was founded, officially recognizing it as an international sport
  • 4
    Kin-Ball World Cup — The World Cup is held regularly, with Canada, Japan, France, and Belgium as strong contenders
  • 5
    Popular in Korean schools — Rapidly spreading as a school sports club activity in Korea, highly valued for its educational emphasis on cooperation and full participation
Rules · Equipment

Equipment and Venue

Learn the facilities and equipment needed for Kin-Ball

  • 1
    Venue — Indoor gym, minimum 20m x 20m. Ceiling height must also be sufficient (for lob hits)
  • 2
    Kin-Ball — Diameter 122cm, weight about 1kg. Latex inner + nylon outer cover. Inflated with an air pump
  • 3
    Team uniforms — Official games: black, grey, and pink. School classes can use vests as substitutes
  • 4
    Air pump — Dedicated Kin-Ball pump. Air pressure affects the ball's bounce and hit distance
Rules

Basic Rules

The unique rule system of Kin-Ball, where 3 teams compete simultaneously

  • 1
    3 teams, 4 players each — Black, grey, and pink teams compete simultaneously on one court. 12 players total in action
  • 2
    Hit & Call — The attacking team hits the ball while calling out another team's color. "Omnikin, Pink!"
  • 3
    Catch mission — The called team must catch the ball before it touches the floor
  • 4
    Scoring rule — If the catch fails, the other two teams each earn 1 point. The team that failed gets 0
  • 5
    Next serve — The team that successfully catches gets the next hit opportunity
Key point — Which team to call is where strategy begins! Do you target the weaker team or keep the stronger team in check?
Rules · Hit

Hit (Serve) Rules

Kin-Ball's unique serving method created by 4 players together

  • 1
    3 of 4 players support the ball — 1 hitter + 3 supporters. The 3 supporters kneel and hold the ball steady
  • 2
    "Omnikin + team color!" call — The call "Omnikin, [color]!" must be shouted loudly before the hit. Hitting without the call is a foul
  • 3
    Hit upward/forward only — Hitting downward or backward is a foul. The hit must go in a direction the opponents can reach
  • 4
    Vary your direction! — Hitting in the same direction repeatedly is a foul. You must use varied directions and tactics
On fouls — If a hit foul occurs, the other two teams each earn 1 point. An accurate hit is true skill!
Rules · Catch

Catch Rules

Before the ball hits the floor, the whole team must work together to catch it!

  • 1
    Catch before it touches the floor — The 4 players of the called team must catch the ball with any body part before it hits the ground
  • 2
    Must secure (control) the ball — The team must control the ball before it hits the floor for a successful catch
  • 3
    Teamwork is key — The giant ball is hard to catch alone. Having all 4 players rush to catch together is the surest method
  • 4
    After a successful catch — The team that catches successfully gets the next hit opportunity. Prepare quickly and attack again!
Remember! — Failed catch = your team gets 0 points while the other two teams get +1 each. One failure benefits two teams!
Skills 1

Hitting Techniques

  • 1
    Basic hit — Strike the ball upward with one arm. The most fundamental hitting technique
  • 2
    Drive hit — A fast, low horizontal strike. The speed makes it difficult to catch
  • 3
    Lob hit — Hit the ball high and far. Looks easy because it gives time to move, but the landing spot is hard to predict
  • 4
    Supporter technique — The skill of 3 players kneeling to hold the ball steady. The ball's height and angle determine the quality of the hit
Skills 2

Catching Techniques

  • 1
    Two-arm catch — The most basic catch. Spread both arms and wrap around the ball
  • 2
    Diving catch — Dive toward a low-flying ball. Essential for responding to drive hits
  • 3
    Team catch — All 4 players gather under the ball and catch together. The most reliable and stable method
  • 4
    Receiving position — Normally, 4 players spread across the gym. This enables quick response to hits from any direction
Tactics · Strategy

Tactics

Strategy is more complex and fun with 3 teams playing simultaneously

⚔️ Offensive Tactics

- Target the weaker team — Strategically call the team with the lowest catch success rate
- Target open spaces — Hit toward areas without opposing players, increasing their travel distance
- Low drive hit — Low, fast hits reduce reaction time and make catching difficult

🛡️ Defensive Tactics

- Quick hit — Hit quickly after a catch before opponents are ready, catching them off guard
- Wide positioning — 4 players spread out to cover the entire gym
- Predict the call — Analyze the opponent's hit patterns to predict which team will be called

Safety

Safety Guidelines

Playing safely with a giant ball — what to watch for

⚠️ Cautions

- Blocked vision — The giant ball can block your view. Watch for collisions with players on the other side
- Diving on gym floor — Diving catches on gym floors can cause friction burns and bruises
- Wrist injuries — Heavy force is applied to wrists during hard hits. Proper technique is essential
- Ensure adequate space — Maintain distance from walls and objects. 12 players running simultaneously requires awareness

🏃 Warm-up

- Wrist/shoulder stretching — Hitting heavily uses wrists and shoulders
- Knee joint warm-up — The supporter role involves repeated kneeling
- Full-body stretching — Diving, running, and direction changes make this a full-body workout
- Light ball practice — Practice ball handling to get comfortable with the Kin-Ball's size

Advanced Skills

Serve Motion Sequence

  • 1
    Supporters ready — 3 players kneel and lift the Kin-Ball steadily with both hands
  • 2
    Hitter positioning — The hitter stands on the opposite side of the intended direction and prepares their approach
  • 3
    Call out — Shout "Omnikin, [color]!" loudly and clearly. Everyone must be able to hear!
  • 4
    Execute the hit — Simultaneously with the call, strike powerfully with one arm (or both). The supporters release the ball and step away
Tips · Etiquette

Game Tips & Etiquette

How to enjoy Kin-Ball more and play it with style

  • 1
    Communication is skill — Constantly communicate with teammates: "I've got it!", "Left!", "Ready!" The 4 players must move as one
  • 2
    Call loudly! — Shout the "Omnikin" call with confidence. It looks great and prevents fouls
  • 3
    Rotate roles — Take turns as hitter and supporter. A strong team means everyone can both hit and support well
  • 4
    Respect all three teams — With 3 teams in play, avoid targeting just one team. Maintain fair play spirit
  • 5
    It's OK to fail! — Missed catches happen often. Encouraging each other and focusing on the next play is real skill
Quiz

True/False Quiz

Let's check what you've learned!

  • Q1
    Kin-Ball is played between 2 teams like other sports. — False. Kin-Ball is the world's only 3-team simultaneous competition sport.
  • Q2
    During a Kin-Ball hit, all 4 team members must touch the ball. — True. 3 supporters hold the ball while 1 hitter strikes. All 4 must be touching the ball at the moment of the hit; otherwise it is a foul.
  • Q3
    When a catch fails, the other two teams each earn 1 point. — True. The failing team gets 0, while the other two teams each get 1 point.
  • Q4
    Before hitting, you must call out "Omnikin + team color." — True. Hitting without the call is a foul.
  • Q5
    The Kin-Ball has a diameter of about 50cm. — False. The Kin-Ball has a diameter of about 122cm (1.22m) — a very large ball.
